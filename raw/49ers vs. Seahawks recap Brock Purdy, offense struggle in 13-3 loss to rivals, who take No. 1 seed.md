### What to Know

- The 49ers (12-4) lost 13-3 to their longtime rival Seahawks (14-3) in Saturday's Week 18 regular-season finale, as Seattle grabbed the NFC West title and the conference's No. 1 playoff seed -- along with its first-round bye.
- After entering the game on a hot streak, Brock Purdy and the 49ers' offense struggled. The QB completed 19 of 27 passes for 127 yards with no touchdowns, one interception and a 64.9 passer rating.
- The 49ers' defense mostly did its job, holding Sam Darnold to no touchdowns on 20-of-26 passing for 198 yards, though the unit did allow Seattle to grab 180 rushing yards and a TD on the ground.
- The Seahawks completely neutralized Christian McCaffrey, who tallied just 23 rushing yards on eight attempts and brought in four catches for 35 receiving yards.
- San Francisco's three points are the team's fewest since a 23-3 loss to the Carolina Panthers in Kyle Shanahan's first game in 2017.
