As Democrats look for a way to rein in President [Donald Trump](https://www.usatoday.com/news/politics/donald-trump/) [amid the war in Iran](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/2026/04/07/trump-ceasefire-reverse-how-happened/89508671007/), Republicans in the House of Representatives blocked them from considering a [measure to halt military hostilities in the region](https://www.usatoday.com/news/iran-war/).

With Congress still on a recess until next week, a war powers resolution, which Democrats planned to offer up on April 9, would have needed unanimous support within the GOP-controlled chamber to move forward. But Rep. Chris Smith, R-New Jersey, refused to recognize Democrats so they could ask to take up the legislation.

He loudly adjourned the House's session in Washington as Democrats protested.

Though the bill was never likely to succeed with Republican leaders standing in the way, it underlined the pressure Democrats on Capitol Hill are feeling to use any political lever to stop what many of them refer to as a "war of choice." Despite [a fragile ceasefire and ongoing negotiations among intermediaries to deescalate](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/world/2026/04/09/trump-ceasefire-lebanon-iran-war-israel-updates--live/89526832007/), Iranian officials continue to rule a vital waterway, the Strait of Hormuz, that has allowed them to hijack oil markets and disrupt the global economy.It wasn't the first time since the U.S. and Israel's Feb. 28 strikes on Iran that GOP lawmakers have prevented Democrats who criticize the war from meaningfully pushing back. But it did mark the first time in recent days – since President Donald Trump threatened to destroy a "whole civilization" in Iran – that Congress acted amid a scheduled two-week vacation for Easter and Passover to respond to the dire situation in the Middle East.

While the April 9 measure didn't proceed, congressional Democrats have signaled they have no intention of letting up in trying to put their Republican colleagues on the record about the conflict. Senate Democrats have promised they'll force a vote on another resolution to stop the war next week.
