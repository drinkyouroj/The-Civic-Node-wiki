The US Federal Reserve kept interest rates unchanged at its March 2026 FOMC meeting, maintaining the federal funds rate at 3.50% to 3.75% while signalling a more cautious outlook for policy easing.

The updated dot plot showed that officials now expect fewer rate cuts in 2026 than previously projected, with the median forecast pointing to only limited easing over the year. This reflects the Fedâs view that economic growth remains resilient and labour market conditions are still firm, reducing the urgency to cut rates in the near term.

At the same time, the Fed revised its inflation projections higher, with core PCE inflation now expected to be around 2.7% in 2026, compared with earlier projections closer to the mid-2% range, and still above the Fedâs 2% target.

Headline PCE inflation was also projected to remain elevated, partly due to higher energy prices and ongoing geopolitical tensions. meaning interest rates may need to stay higher for longer as a result.

## Impact of US Fed interest rate decision on Singapore investors

Here's how your savings and investments may be impacted when the US Fed cuts interest rates.

Savings Accounts & Fixed Deposits: Banks tend to lower interest rates on savings accounts and fixed deposits, resulting in lower returns for depositors.

Singapore T-Bills: Yields on US Treasuries typically decline with rate cuts, and Singapore T-bill yields often follow, offering lower potential returns for investors.

Singapore REITs: Lower interest rates reduce borrowing costs for REITs, improving their profitability. Additionally, with declining yields on fixed-income assets, investors may shift towards REITs for higher dividend yields, boosting demand and potentially lifting their share prices.

Bond Funds: Lower interest rates boost the prices of existing bonds, as newer bonds offer lower yields. This leads to potential capital gains for bond funds, enhancing their overall returns.

## How does US Fed affect the interest rates?

The US Federal Reserve sets the US Fed interest rate, which directly influences economic activity and financial markets. Through its FOMC meetings, the US Fed adjusts interest rates to manage inflation, encourage economic growth, or stabilize financial markets. A Fed interest rate hike or rate cut can affect everything from mortgage rates to investment returns, including global markets like Singapore. Understanding how the US Fed interest rate moves helps investors anticipate changes in the market and adjust their portfolios accordingly.

Why Does the Fed Change Interest Rates?

- To Control Inflation: Higher rates slow down inflation by making borrowing more expensive.
- To Stimulate Growth: Lower rates encourage spending and investment, boosting the economy.
- To Maintain Stability: The Fed adjusts rates to balance economic growth and financial stability.

The CME FedWatch Tool helps investors anticipate these Fed rate changes by providing real-time probability forecasts ahead of each FOMC meeting.
