SAN FRANCISCO, Cal. (WBAY) - The former Packers personnel men are now set to square off in the Super Bowl. The Patriots’ Eliot Wolf and the Seahawks’ John Schneider.

Schneider won Executive of the Year in the NFL, now in his 16th season with Seattle. That’s in eternity in NFL terms. Schneider has become the first GM ever to lead teams to the Super Bowl with not a single holdover on the player side or coaching side.

“Don’t be fooled by his awe shucks personality,” said Seahawks head coach Mike MacDonald. “He’s as sharp as they come, and he seems like he’s young because of his energy and his enthusiasm. But he’s seen a lot of football. He’s seen a lot of great organizations operate. He’s got a very clear vision on what he wants to achieve. Man, what a great communicator.

“The spirit behind how he operates is, is the Seahawks. You know, the brand of the Seahawks. John exemplifies that every day. Man, he is a awesome person to work with on a day to day basis. I look forward to seeing him every day.

“He makes he makes it fun, he makes it exciting. He challenges you a lot. I had a lot of mentors going through the process two years ago, and to a man said, you go work with that guy. That meant a lot, and he’s lived up to that. And then some.”

And it’s not just longevity getting the job done. Schneider has made some really big moves. He won a Super Bowl with a quarterback on a rookie contract, but went out and signed Sam Darnold this time around. This season Seattle also cut ties with longtime receivers D.K. Metcalf and Tyler Lockett. They just keep turning over the roster, drafting well and reaching the big stage yet again.
