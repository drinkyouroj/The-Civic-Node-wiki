Oracle and OpenAI have cancelled plans to expand their flagship AI data center campus in Abilene, Texas, after lengthy negotiations broke down over financing arrangements and OpenAI's changing capacity projections.

What was cancelled: The expansion was originally intended to scale from an already massive 1.2 gigawatts (GW) to roughly 2.0 GW, representing a 600 MW expansion — one of the largest AI infrastructure reversals on record.

Why it failed: Two forces killed the expansion: financing negotiations that dragged without resolution, and a shift in OpenAI's demand forecasts that made the additional capacity harder to justify.

What remains: Development of the 1,000-acre campus remains underway, and multiple facilities are already in service, though preliminary agreements to rent a substantial expansion were ultimately dropped. Oracle agreed to develop 4.5 GW of data center capacity for OpenAI, and that program continues.

New developments: Microsoft is taking over the data center construction project in Texas after OpenAI declined to pursue it. Crusoe is building two new "AI factory" buildings and an on-site power plant in Abilene. Meta is reportedly in talks to pick up excess Crusoe capacity with Nvidia's help.
