China's two major memory manufacturers, CXMT (ChangXin Memory Technologies) and YMTC (Yangtze Memory Technologies Corp), view the global supply shortage as an opportunity for "emerging players" to catch up and are embarking on record-level expansion.

CXMT's Shanghai expansion:
- Preparing a major DRAM facility in Shanghai expected to be 2-3x larger than its existing Hefei headquarters
- Equipment and installation beginning later this year
- Volume production targeted for 2027
- CXMT intends to add dedicated HBM3 production lines as part of its Shanghai fab
- Initial HBM3 volumes aimed at domestic AI accelerators
- 2025 revenue surged 130% year-over-year, exceeding 55 billion yuan (~$8 billion)
- Filed IPO application to Shanghai Stock Exchange, aiming to raise RMB 29.5 billion ($4.1 billion)
- Currently the world's fourth-largest DRAM maker following Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron
- Market share by production capacity: ~11.1% (2025), projected ~13.9% by 2027
- Yole estimates wafer capacity share: ~12% in 2025, could expand to 15% by 2028

YMTC's Wuhan expansion:
- Constructing a third fab in Wuhan, coming online around 2027
- Roughly half of that facility's planned output expected to be dedicated to DRAM rather than NAND — a significant deviation from YMTC's traditional NAND focus
- Planning to go public in the second half of 2026, with proceeds directed toward equipment and R&D

Market context:
- Samsung and SK Hynix have warned customers that memory supply tightness is likely to persist into 2027
- Chinese manufacturers are leveraging the shortage to catch up while established players focus on AI memory (HBM)
- The expansion could contribute to a price glut by 2028 as both Chinese and Korean new capacity comes online simultaneously
