## GENIUS Act Stablecoins

Here's the thing about the GENIUS Act stablecoins:

- It mandates full fiat backing plus audited treasury reserves.
- Issuers must hold a federal banking charter.
- Consumer protection and anti-money laundering are central.
- That creates a high-trust legal setting for stablecoin use in global payments.

What this really means is the GENIUS Act stablecoins become more like regulated digital dollars, providing well-structured and safer for cross-border movement. TransFi supports all GENIUS Act stablecoins so your global payments remain legal, transparent, and predictable.

## EU MiCA Crypto Regulation

MiCA regulation EU sets a clear standard for stablecoin compliance standards across the continent. Here are some important points to note:

- MiCA regulation EU requires all stablecoin issuers to be licensed and maintain reserve audits.
- It limits algorithmic stablecoins and enforces disclosure of redemption policies.
- Issuers must also follow consumer protection, liquidity, and governance rules.

TransFi stays compliant by working only with MiCA-approved stablecoins across the continent and managing compliance for transfers and settlement across Europe.

## Global Stablecoin Laws

Not every country follows MiCA or the GENIUS Act. Many Asian nations prefer frameworks that let experimentation coexist with regulation. That’s why global stablecoin laws vary widely.

- Europe uses MiCA regulation EU to build across-member-state consistency.
- Asian markets prefer sandbox-style Asia crypto regulation with Singapore, Japan, and Hong Kong each offer different levels of openness.

What this really means is market entry in each region depends on local legal frameworks, requiring adaptable platforms and compliant engineering.
