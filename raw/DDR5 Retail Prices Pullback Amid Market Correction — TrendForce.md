DDR5 retail prices have experienced sharp corrections globally. In Germany, prices fell 7.2% month-on-month in March 2026, marking the first decline after eight consecutive months of gains. However, prices remained approximately 408% higher than July 2025 levels.

The U.S. market mirrors this trend. Corsair's VENGEANCE 32GB DDR5 kit dropped to around $379.99, representing a 20%+ decline from its recent $490 peak.

China's e-commerce platforms showed significant pullback: mainstream 16GB DDR5-5600/6000 modules fell from approximately RMB 1,300 (January-February) to roughly RMB 1,000, a 25-30% cumulative decline. Meanwhile, 32GB kits eased from RMB 3,800 to around RMB 3,200, with some domestic brands falling below RMB 3,000.

Shenzhen's Huaqiangbei Electronics Market witnessed aggressive inventory liquidation, with 32GB modules dropping from near RMB 3,000 to as low as RMB 1,950 in fire-sale pricing.

Despite retail volatility, Taiwan-based memory suppliers maintained completely stable contract pricing. Industry sources emphasized "no need for concern," noting that spot market fluctuations typically filter through to actual shipments with a one- to two-month lag.

Taiwan-based module makers are maintaining strict pricing discipline while original vendors continue restricting DDR4 capacity. Demand remains sticky in industrial control segments where substitution is limited.

The correction appears consumer-driven and short-term rather than signaling structural demand deterioration. A module business executive attributed the sell-off to softer near-term build demand and accelerated inventory digestion, emphasizing the broader memory cycle uptrend remains unchanged.

Analyst commentary suggests memory prices surged over 300% from late 2025 through early 2026, triggering aggressive stockpiling, with DDR5 16GB modules potentially normalizing by year-end 2026.
