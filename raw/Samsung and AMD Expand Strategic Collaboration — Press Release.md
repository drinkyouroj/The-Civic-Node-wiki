Samsung Electronics and AMD signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) on March 18, 2026, to expand their strategic collaboration on next-generation AI memory and computing technologies.

The signing ceremony was held at Samsung's most advanced chip manufacturing complex in Pyeongtaek, Korea, with Dr. Lisa Su (AMD CEO) and Samsung Electronics Vice Chairman & CEO Young Hyun Jun present.

Deal specifics:
- Samsung designated as primary HBM4 supplier for AMD Instinct MI455X GPU (next-generation AI accelerator)
- Advanced DRAM solutions for 6th Gen AMD EPYC CPUs (codenamed "Venice")
- High-performance DDR5 memory optimized for 6th Gen AMD EPYC CPUs

Samsung's HBM4 specifications:
- Built on 6th-generation 10-nanometer-class DRAM process
- 4nm logic base die
- Processing speeds of up to 13 gigabits-per-second (Gbps)
- Maximum 3.3 terabytes-per-second (TB/s) bandwidth

Context: AMD CEO Lisa Su's visit was part of a broader South Korea trip that also included meetings with Naver. The visit represents AMD's deepening engagement with Korean memory supply chains as it competes with Nvidia in the AI accelerator market.

Reports indicate Lisa Su also held a private meeting with Samsung Vice Chairman Lee Jae-yong (Jay Y. Lee) during the trip, signaling the deal's strategic importance at the highest levels.
