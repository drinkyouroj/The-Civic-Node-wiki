OpenAI has substantially scaled back its capital expenditure plans, signaling a major shift in strategy. During a November podcast appearance, CEO Sam Altman became defensive when investor Grad Gerstner questioned how the company could commit to $1.4 trillion in spending on revenues of just $13 billion annually. Altman responded dismissively: "If you want to sell your shares, I'll find you a buyer. Enough."

The company subsequently informed investors it now targets approximately $600 billion in total compute spending by 2030 — less than half its previous commitment. This represents a significant reversal as investor confidence in massive AI spending plans has deteriorated.

Key financial figures:
- Original commitment: $1.4 trillion through 2030
- New target: $600 billion through 2030 (57% reduction)
- 2025 revenue: $13.1 billion
- 2025 burn rate: $8 billion

Major competitors including Google have established revenue streams supporting AI investments, while tech giants like Amazon and Microsoft experienced stock declines after reaffirming vast spending commitments.

Altman previously declared "code red" status, directing employees to prioritize ChatGPT development. Additionally, OpenAI plans to introduce advertising within its chatbot platform — a move competitors have criticized sharply.

The article concludes with an anecdote about Altman and Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei refusing to hold hands during an AI Summit in New Delhi, India.
