![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Tom_Brady_and_Bill_Belichick.jpg/250px-Tom_Brady_and_Bill_Belichick.jpg)

Brady and Belichick, pictured in 2009

The **Tom Brady–Bill Belichick era**, also known as the **Brady–Belichick era**, the **New England Patriots dynasty**, or the **Patriots dynasty**, was a [sports dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sports_dynasty "Sports dynasty") of the [New England Patriots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England_Patriots "New England Patriots") in the [National Football League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Football_League "National Football League") (NFL) that lasted from the [2000](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000_NFL_season "2000 NFL season") to the [2019 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_NFL_season "2019 NFL season"). The dynasty is named after quarterback [Tom Brady](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Brady "Tom Brady") and head coach [Bill Belichick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_Belichick "Bill Belichick"), who are regarded as among the greatest in their respective positions. Belichick and Brady are generally credited with the Patriots' success [^2] [^3] [^4] [^5] [^6] [^7] and for creating one of the sport's longest and most dominant dynasties.[^8] [^9] They are also credited with helping to create a team culture, dubbed the "Patriot Way", that emphasizes personal accountability, consistent improvement, and team success over personal gain.

Under Belichick and Brady, the Patriots played in nine [Super Bowls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl "Super Bowl"), more than any other NFL franchise, and won a league-record six (tied with the [Pittsburgh Steelers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pittsburgh_Steelers "Pittsburgh Steelers")). They also had 19 consecutive winning seasons, 17 division titles (including 11 consecutive from [2009](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_New_England_Patriots_season "2009 New England Patriots season") to [2019](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_New_England_Patriots_season "2019 New England Patriots season")), 13 [AFC Championship Game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AFC_Championship_Game "AFC Championship Game") appearances (including eight consecutive from [2011](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_New_England_Patriots_season "2011 New England Patriots season") to [2018](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_New_England_Patriots_season "2018 New England Patriots season")), and the league’s only undefeated 16-game regular season in [2007](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_New_England_Patriots_season "2007 New England Patriots season").[^10] The Patriots appeared in half of all Super Bowls played during Brady's 18 seasons as the primary starter [^1] and won two-thirds of those.

In addition to helping set the Patriots' franchise records, Brady and Belichick hold the records for the most Super Bowl appearances and victories by a player and head coach, respectively. Belichick also holds the records for the most playoff wins by a head coach (31) and Super Bowl appearances by an individual (12), while Brady holds the quarterback records for career wins (264), regular season wins (230), and playoff wins (34) and the record for [Super Bowl MVP awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_Most_Valuable_Player_Award "Super Bowl Most Valuable Player Award") (five).

The era ended when Brady left the Patriots as a free agent for the [Tampa Bay Buccaneers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Buccaneers "Tampa Bay Buccaneers") in March 2020, where he went on to win a 7th [Super Bowl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LV "Super Bowl LV"). Belichick remained as head coach of the Patriots until the end of the [2023 NFL season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_NFL_season "2023 NFL season"), which coincided with Brady's retirement from the sport.

## Bill Belichick

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Bill_Belichick_2019_crop.jpg/250px-Bill_Belichick_2019_crop.jpg)

Bill Belichick was the head coach of the Patriots from 2000 to 2023

Belichick joined the Patriots in 1996 as an assistant head coach and defensive backs coach for [Bill Parcells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_Parcells "Bill Parcells"). He previously spent 13 years with the [New York Giants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Giants "New York Giants") from 1979 to 1990. During this period, he was the defensive coordinator during the Giants' 39–20 victory over the [Denver Broncos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denver_Broncos "Denver Broncos") in [Super Bowl XXI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXI "Super Bowl XXI") and 20–19 victory over the [Buffalo Bills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buffalo_Bills "Buffalo Bills") in [Super Bowl XXV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXV "Super Bowl XXV").

The Patriots went 11–5 in 1996 and earned a trip to [Super Bowl XXXI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXXI "Super Bowl XXXI"), but lost to the [Green Bay Packers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Bay_Packers "Green Bay Packers"). Belichick subsequently served as assistant head coach and defensive coordinator for the [New York Jets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Jets "New York Jets"). When Parcells, then their head coach, retired in 1999, Belichick was supposed to be his successor, but resigned abruptly and went to [New England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England_Patriots "New England Patriots") instead, this time as their head coach. During his tenure in New England, the Patriots missed the playoffs only six times: 2000 (5–11), 2002 (9–7), 2008 (11–5), 2020 (7–9), 2022 (8–9), and 2023 (4–13). In 2008, Belichick coached the Patriots to an 11–5 record despite losing franchise quarterback Tom Brady in Week 1 versus the [Kansas City Chiefs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kansas_City_Chiefs "Kansas City Chiefs"). A series of division tiebreakers left the 11–5 [Miami Dolphins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami_Dolphins "Miami Dolphins") as [AFC East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AFC_East "AFC East") champions, making the 2008 Patriots the second team in NFL history to go 11–5 and miss the playoffs.

In 2010, he was selected as the first-team head coach in the [National Football League 2000s All-Decade Team](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Football_League_2000s_All-Decade_Team "National Football League 2000s All-Decade Team"). He has won the AP Coach of the Year award three times in his career, all during his time with the Patriots. As head coach, Belichick is 225–79 (.740 winning percentage) in regular season games and has coached the Patriots to 40 postseason games, winning 30. From 2010 to 2017, Belichick coached the Patriots to 8 consecutive 12+ win seasons, an NFL record. The Patriots, since 2002, have lost more than 5 games just three times. As the Patriots head coach, Belichick has had just four losing seasons, in 2000, 2020, 2022 and 2023. In 21 seasons from 2001 onwards, the Patriots have won 16 AFC East titles and earned a wild-card berth in 2021. Such dominance in the salary cap era, which limits teams in how much they can pay players and therefore forces parity, is credited to Belichick's football prowess.

Belichick is known for taking players who had lackluster or declining careers and making them productive for one or more seasons, including RB [Corey Dillon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corey_Dillon "Corey Dillon"), who gained 1,738 yards from scrimmage, including 1,635 rushing yards.[^13] Hall of Fame wide receiver [Randy Moss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randy_Moss "Randy Moss") was traded to New England in 2007 after lackluster play for the [Oakland Raiders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oakland_Raiders "Oakland Raiders"), but went on to catch 23 touchdowns in 2007, setting an NFL record. Belichick drafted wide receiver [Julian Edelman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Edelman "Julian Edelman") in the 7th round. Edelman went on to set a franchise record for postseason receptions [^14] and caught one of the most memorable catches in [Super Bowl LI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LI "Super Bowl LI"). He also earned a [Super Bowl MVP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_Most_Valuable_Player_Award "Super Bowl Most Valuable Player Award") award [two years later](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LIII "Super Bowl LIII"). Most notably, Belichick is credited with drafting Brady with the 199th pick in the 2000 NFL Draft. Brady went on to help the Patriots form a [dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sports_dynasty "Sports dynasty") that lasted from 2001 until 2019, and currently holds every NFL postseason passing record except yards per attempt and completion percentage.

Belichick is third all-time for wins as a coach.[^15] He was the longest tenured active coach in the NFL when he left the Patriots. He has the most postseason wins, with 31.[^15] He is the only coach to win three Super Bowls in four years and the only head coach to have won six Super Bowls.[^15]

## Tom Brady

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Tom_Brady_2019.jpg/250px-Tom_Brady_2019.jpg)

Quarterback Tom Brady was the Patriots' primary starter for 18 seasons

Brady attended college at the [University of Michigan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Michigan "University of Michigan"), where he battled for the starting role with [Brian Griese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Griese "Brian Griese") and [Drew Henson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drew_Henson "Drew Henson"). He led the Wolverines to the 2000 Orange Bowl, with a record 10–2 as starter during the regular season. Despite trailing by two scores twice during the game, Brady led the Wolverines to an overtime win over the Alabama Crimson Tide, 35–34. His come-from-behind victory earned him the nickname "Comeback Kid".[^16]

Due to questions about his starting ability in college and uninspiring physical measurables, Brady fell in the [2000 draft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000_NFL_draft "2000 NFL draft") until he was selected in the 6th round by the New England Patriots. He spent his rookie year as backup to franchise quarterback [Drew Bledsoe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drew_Bledsoe "Drew Bledsoe") and would not start a game until the next year when Bledsoe, who had signed a 10-year, $103 million contract that offseason, was seriously injured by a sideline hit by Jets linebacker [Mo Lewis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mo_Lewis "Mo Lewis") on September 23, 2001.

Brady went 11–3 as a starter in 2001, leading the Patriots to the Super Bowl against the [St. Louis Rams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001_St._Louis_Rams_season "2001 St. Louis Rams season"). After trailing 17–3 in the second half, the Rams stormed back to tie the game 17–17 with 1:30 left in the game. With no timeouts, the Patriots drove downfield to set up a 48-yard field goal by [Adam Vinatieri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adam_Vinatieri "Adam Vinatieri") to win the game in the closing seconds.[^17]

Brady holds the NFL postseason record for appearances, wins, pass attempts, completions, yards, and touchdowns. In the Super Bowl, Brady holds the record for most pass attempts, completions, touchdowns, and yards. In [Super Bowl LI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LI "Super Bowl LI") he helped the Patriots, who were down 28–3 in the third quarter against the [Atlanta Falcons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlanta_Falcons "Atlanta Falcons"), score 31 unanswered points and win the game in overtime, 34–28, and set a Super Bowl record with 466 passing yards. The following year, he broke his own record by throwing for 505 yards in a loss to the [Philadelphia Eagles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philadelphia_Eagles "Philadelphia Eagles").

Brady holds every Super Bowl record in passing except for completion percentage and interceptions. In his 10 appearances in 22 years, he has come away with 7 wins and 5 Super Bowl MVPs (including 1 appearance, win and MVP with the [Tampa Bay Buccaneers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Buccaneers "Tampa Bay Buccaneers")).

With the Patriots, Brady threw touchdowns to 77 players, breaking the NFL record of 70 held by [Vinny Testaverde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinny_Testaverde "Vinny Testaverde").[^18] [^19] By retirement, he had extended his record to 96.[^20]

## Brady–Belichick era

In the [2000 NFL draft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000_NFL_draft "2000 NFL draft"), first year head coach [Bill Belichick](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_Belichick "Bill Belichick") was looking for ways to improve a New England Patriots team that had not been to a Super Bowl since [1996](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1996_New_England_Patriots_season "1996 New England Patriots season"). Belichick had been hired after 3rd-year head coach [Pete Carroll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pete_Carroll "Pete Carroll") was fired following the [1999 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1999_New_England_Patriots_season "1999 New England Patriots season"). Before the draft the Patriots sent quarterbacks coach [Dick Rehbein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dick_Rehbein "Dick Rehbein") to scout quarterbacks for the draft. When Rehbein returned, he stated that Brady was the best quarterback prospect for the Patriots' system.[^21] Despite the Patriots already having a franchise quarterback in Drew Bledsoe, they drafted Tom Brady in the sixth round.

### 2000–2002: Beginnings and first Super Bowl

The Patriots went 5–11 in [2000](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000_New_England_Patriots_season "2000 New England Patriots season"), the last losing season the Patriots had until 2020. Belichick, after a loss during the season, claimed that "I can't win games with 40 good players while the other team has 53" after many players showed up to camp out of condition.[^22]

In [2001](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001_New_England_Patriots_season "2001 New England Patriots season"), Belichick made major reforms. The beginning of the culture he brought to New England, dubbed the "Patriot Way", was beginning to show with wide receiver [Terry Glenn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terry_Glenn "Terry Glenn") 's contract holdout. The Patriots could not afford to raise Glenn's salary, and Belichick suspended Glenn indefinitely after repeated failed drug tests. Glenn did not receive a ring when the Patriots won [Super Bowl XXXVI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXXVI "Super Bowl XXXVI") that year.[^23]

The Patriots started 0–1, and faced a division rival in Week 2 against the [New York Jets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001_New_York_Jets_season "2001 New York Jets season"). Late in the game, down 10–3, quarterback Drew Bledsoe rolled out of the pocket and tried to scramble down the sideline, until he was hit by Jets linebacker [Mo Lewis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mo_Lewis "Mo Lewis"). In the hospital later that day, Bledsoe came perilously close to death resulting from internal bleeding sustained in the hit. The concussion alone had been more than enough to sideline him for the remainder of the game. The Patriots lost the game, moving to 0–2. Second year backup QB Tom Brady started the next game against the [Indianapolis Colts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001_Indianapolis_Colts_season "2001 Indianapolis Colts season"), the first of 17 games in the famous [Tom Brady–Peyton Manning rivalry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Brady%E2%80%93Peyton_Manning_rivalry "Tom Brady–Peyton Manning rivalry"). Brady landed the opening salvo, taking the first of his 11 wins in the series, including two playoff games. The Patriots finished the season on an 11–3 run to go 11–5 overall. The playoffs saw New England beat the [Oakland Raiders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001_Oakland_Raiders_season "2001 Oakland Raiders season") in the [Tuck Rule Game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuck_Rule_Game "Tuck Rule Game") that closed [Foxboro Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foxboro_Stadium "Foxboro Stadium"), then the [Pittsburgh Steelers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001_Pittsburgh_Steelers_season "2001 Pittsburgh Steelers season") on the road; they ended the season with an upset of the heavily favored [St. Louis Rams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2001_St._Louis_Rams_season "2001 St. Louis Rams season") in [Super Bowl XXXVI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXXVI "Super Bowl XXXVI"), 20–17.

The Patriots started off [2002](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2002_New_England_Patriots_season "2002 New England Patriots season") hot, winning their first three games to open up the new [Gillette Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gillette_Stadium "Gillette Stadium"), but losing the next four. They ended with a 9–7 record, but a series of division and conference tiebreakers forced the Patriots out of the playoffs. It was one of only two times in the Brady–Belichick era that the Patriots missed the playoffs, and the only time with Brady as their starting quarterback. (They also missed the playoffs in 2008, when Matt Cassel replaced an injured Brady.)

### 2003–04: Back-to-back Super Bowls

Ahead of the [2003 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003_New_England_Patriots_season "2003 New England Patriots season"), Bill Belichick surprisingly released strong safety [Lawyer Milloy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lawyer_Milloy "Lawyer Milloy") in the offseason due to contract issues. As a replacement he signed hard-hitting enforcer [Rodney Harrison](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rodney_Harrison "Rodney Harrison"), whose career appeared to be on the decline until that point. By the time Harrison retired in 2008, he held the NFL record for most sacks by a defensive back (30.5) and became the first defensive player to have 30 sacks and 30 interceptions; he also holds the Patriots' team record for postseason interceptions, with seven.

The Patriots lost their season opener 31–0 to the [Buffalo Bills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003_Buffalo_Bills_season "2003 Buffalo Bills season"), who had signed Milloy days earlier. It was the worst loss of the Brady–Belichick era. What followed has been called one of the most successful two year stretches by a team in NFL history. The Patriots lost just three more games in 2003 and [2004](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2004_New_England_Patriots_season "2004 New England Patriots season") combined, culminating in back-to-back Super Bowl victories (Super Bowls [XXXVIII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXXVIII "Super Bowl XXXVIII") over the [Carolina Panthers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003_Carolina_Panthers_season "2003 Carolina Panthers season") and [XXXIX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XXXIX "Super Bowl XXXIX") over the [Philadelphia Eagles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2004_Philadelphia_Eagles_season "2004 Philadelphia Eagles season")). In 2004, they set the then-NFL record for consecutive victories, from Week 5, 2003 through Week 7, 2004. Until the [2023 Kansas City Chiefs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Kansas_City_Chiefs_season "2023 Kansas City Chiefs season"), the 2003–04 Patriots were the last team to repeat as Super Bowl champions.

### 2005–06: Playoff disappointments

[2005](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005_New_England_Patriots_season "2005 New England Patriots season") was somewhat of a down year for the Patriots as they finished with a 10–6 record. The 2005 season featured injuries to many key players including safety Rodney Harrison and forced the Patriots to start 45 different players throughout the season, an NFL record. Linebacker Tedy Bruschi suffered a stroke and missed the beginning of the season before returning for the October 30 game against the [Buffalo Bills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005_Buffalo_Bills_season "2005 Buffalo Bills season"). Despite the injuries, the Patriots won the AFC East for the third straight time. They defeated the [Jacksonville Jaguars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005_Jacksonville_Jaguars_season "2005 Jacksonville Jaguars season") in the Wild Card round, but lost to the [Denver Broncos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005_Denver_Broncos_season "2005 Denver Broncos season") in the Divisional round, committing five turnovers.

In [2006](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2006_New_England_Patriots_season "2006 New England Patriots season"), the Patriots came back strong, going 12–4 and winning the AFC East again. After beating the [New York Jets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2006_New_York_Jets_season "2006 New York Jets season") and [San Diego Chargers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2006_San_Diego_Chargers_season "2006 San Diego Chargers season") in the wildcard and divisional rounds, they squandered a 21–3 halftime lead against the [Indianapolis Colts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2006_Indianapolis_Colts_season "2006 Indianapolis Colts season") in the AFC Championship Game and lost 38–34.

### 2007: 16–0 record, close to perfection

In [2007](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_New_England_Patriots_season "2007 New England Patriots season"), the Patriots became the first team to win every regular season game since the NFL schedule was extended to 16 games. They outscored opposing teams by an average of 36–17. Brady won MVP for the first time in his career, throwing for 4800 yards and an NFL record 50 touchdowns.[^24] Wide receiver [Randy Moss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randy_Moss "Randy Moss"), traded to New England from the [Oakland Raiders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oakland_Raiders "Oakland Raiders") for a 4th round pick, caught an NFL record 23 touchdown passes, a record that still stands.[^24] After beating the [Jacksonville Jaguars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_Jacksonville_Jaguars_season "2007 Jacksonville Jaguars season") and [San Diego Chargers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_San_Diego_Chargers_season "2007 San Diego Chargers season") in the playoffs, New England suffered their first defeat of the season at the hands of the [New York Giants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_New_York_Giants_season "2007 New York Giants season") in [Super Bowl XLII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLII "Super Bowl XLII"), 17–14, the same team New England had beaten in week 17 to cap the 16–0 record. Super Bowl XLII became renowned for the miraculous play made by [Eli Manning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eli_Manning "Eli Manning") and [David Tyree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Tyree "David Tyree"), which became known as the [Helmet Catch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helmet_Catch "Helmet Catch").[^24] With the loss, the Patriots failed to match the [1972 Miami Dolphins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1972_Miami_Dolphins_season "1972 Miami Dolphins season"), the only team in NFL history to complete a perfect season (regular season and playoffs).

### 2008–2010: Brady's injury and more playoff disappointments

The [2008](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_New_England_Patriots_season "2008 New England Patriots season") season started off with disaster for the Patriots, when Brady suffered a season-ending knee injury in Week 1 versus the [Kansas City Chiefs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_Kansas_City_Chiefs_season "2008 Kansas City Chiefs season"). Despite the loss of Brady, Belichick coached the Patriots, with backup quarterback [Matt Cassel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matt_Cassel "Matt Cassel") under center, to a respectable 11–5 record. A series of division and conference tiebreakers made the 11–5 [Miami Dolphins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_Miami_Dolphins_season "2008 Miami Dolphins season") AFC East champions and allowed the 11–5 [Baltimore Ravens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_Baltimore_Ravens_season "2008 Baltimore Ravens season") to clinch the sixth and final conference playoff berth. The 2008 Patriots became the second 11-win team in NFL history to not make the playoffs (the other being the [1985 Denver Broncos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1985_Denver_Broncos_season "1985 Denver Broncos season")).

The [2009](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_New_England_Patriots_season "2009 New England Patriots season") season was important for several reasons. They started the year with a comeback against the [Buffalo Bills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_Buffalo_Bills_season "2009 Buffalo Bills season") to go 1–0. In week 6 the Patriots beat the [Tennessee Titans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_Tennessee_Titans_season "2009 Tennessee Titans season") 59–0, tying the NFL record for the largest blowout since the 1970 merger. After a string of losses, including a heartbreaking road loss to the undefeated [Indianapolis Colts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_Indianapolis_Colts_season "2009 Indianapolis Colts season"), the Patriots finished 10–6 but lost to the [Baltimore Ravens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_Baltimore_Ravens_season "2009 Baltimore Ravens season") 33–14 in the Wild Card round. Brady had a comeback year, throwing for 4,300 yards and 28 touchdowns.

In [2010](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010_New_England_Patriots_season "2010 New England Patriots season"), the Patriots went 14–2 and won the AFC East outright. Brady became the first unanimous NFL MVP, as well as the Offensive Player of the year. Despite all of the regular season hype, the Patriots failed to live up to it in the playoffs, losing in the Divisional Round to the division rival [New York Jets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010_New_York_Jets_season "2010 New York Jets season") 28–21.

### 2011–2013: Super Bowl loss and consecutive losses in the AFC Championship Game

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Carolina_Panthers_salute_service_members_131118-F-CX352-0313.jpg/250px-Carolina_Panthers_salute_service_members_131118-F-CX352-0313.jpg)

Brady attempts a pass against the Carolina Panthers in 2013

In [2011](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_New_England_Patriots_season "2011 New England Patriots season"), the Patriots had another strong year, finishing with a 13–3 record, winning the AFC East, and making it to the Super Bowl. Tom Brady threw for 5,235 yards, still short of the NFL single-season record which was set by [Drew Brees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drew_Brees "Drew Brees") in the same season. In [Super Bowl XLVI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLVI "Super Bowl XLVI"), against the [New York Giants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_New_York_Giants_season "2011 New York Giants season"), Brady was pressured again and had difficulty scoring points. Another miracle play for the Giants offense made by Eli Manning on a pass to Mario Manningham allowed the Giants offense to take a late lead over the Patriots in the fourth quarter. The Patriots again lost to Manning and the Giants, 21–17.

For the [2012 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2012_New_England_Patriots_season "2012 New England Patriots season"), Brady threw for 4,827 yards and rushing for a career-high 4 touchdowns. After beating the [Houston Texans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2012_Houston_Texans_season "2012 Houston Texans season") in the Divisional round, they lost to the [Baltimore Ravens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2012_Baltimore_Ravens_season "2012 Baltimore Ravens season") in the AFC Championship Game 28–13.

Brady and Belichick led the [2013](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_New_England_Patriots_season "2013 New England Patriots season") Patriots team to a 12–4 record, earning the team a first round playoff bye week for the fourth year in a row even despite Brady only throwing 25 touchdowns and adding 11 interceptions. The premier victory of the Patriots regular season came in Week 6 against the [New Orleans Saints](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_New_Orleans_Saints_season "2013 New Orleans Saints season"). Despite consecutive Patriots drives ending in a 3-and-out and Brady throwing a seemingly game-ending interception with 2:16 left in the fourth quarter, the Patriots defense forced the Saints to punt with 1:13 left in the game. Brady then led the Patriots to score with only 5 seconds left, throwing a touchdown to [Kenbrell Thompkins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenbrell_Thompkins "Kenbrell Thompkins") in the corner of the end zone. In the playoffs, the Patriots easily made it to the AFC Championship Game, where they faced the [Denver Broncos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_Denver_Broncos_season "2013 Denver Broncos season") and their record-breaking offense. In another [Tom Brady–Peyton Manning rivalry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Brady%E2%80%93Peyton_Manning_rivalry "Tom Brady–Peyton Manning rivalry") game, the Broncos won 26–16.

### 2014: Fourth Super Bowl victory

The Patriots began the [2014 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_New_England_Patriots_season "2014 New England Patriots season") with a rough 2–2 record, including an embarrassing loss to the [Kansas City Chiefs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Kansas_City_Chiefs_season "2014 Kansas City Chiefs season") in Week 4. Many sports writers were beginning to call for the end of the Patriots dynasty, which had not won a Super Bowl since the 2004 season. Still, the Patriots bounced back and went 10–2 down the stretch to finish the season 12–4. After coming back from two 14 point deficits to beat the [Baltimore Ravens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Baltimore_Ravens_season "2014 Baltimore Ravens season") in the divisional round, the Patriots routed the [Indianapolis Colts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Indianapolis_Colts_season "2014 Indianapolis Colts season") 45–7 in the AFC Championship Game, earning their sixth Super Bowl appearance since 2001.

In [Super Bowl XLIX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLIX "Super Bowl XLIX"), after throwing two interceptions and leaving his team down by 10 points in the 4th quarter, Brady led the Patriots on two touchdown drives against the [Seattle Seahawks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Seattle_Seahawks_season "2014 Seattle Seahawks season"). The Patriots capped off the comeback with a touchdown pass to [Julian Edelman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Edelman "Julian Edelman") with just over two minutes remaining in regulation. On the ensuing Seahawks drive, quarterback [Russell Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russell_Wilson "Russell Wilson") threw a deep pass to wide receiver [Jermaine Kearse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jermaine_Kearse "Jermaine Kearse") that was initially deflected by cornerback [Malcolm Butler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malcolm_Butler "Malcolm Butler"). Kearse juggled the ball on his chest and caught it, giving Seattle a first down on the 5 yard line. Color commentator [Cris Collinsworth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cris_Collinsworth "Cris Collinsworth") compared the acrobatic catch to [David Tyree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Tyree "David Tyree") 's iconic [Helmet Catch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helmet_Catch "Helmet Catch") against the Patriots in [Super Bowl XLII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_XLII "Super Bowl XLII"). On the next play, running back [Marshawn Lynch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshawn_Lynch "Marshawn Lynch") rushed to the 1 yard line, where he was tackled by [Dont'a Hightower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dont%27a_Hightower "Dont'a Hightower"). Then with 26 seconds left in the game, Wilson's pass for [Ricardo Lockette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ricardo_Lockette "Ricardo Lockette") was intercepted by undrafted rookie cornerback [Malcolm Butler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malcolm_Butler "Malcolm Butler") on the goal line. The Patriots prevailed, 28–24.

### 2015: AFC Championship loss

The Patriots had a hot start to the [2015 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_New_England_Patriots_season "2015 New England Patriots season"), winning their first ten games. A series of injuries, including [Julian Edelman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Edelman "Julian Edelman"), [Rob Gronkowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rob_Gronkowski "Rob Gronkowski"), [Brandon LaFell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brandon_LaFell "Brandon LaFell"), [Dion Lewis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dion_Lewis "Dion Lewis"), and at one point, the entire offensive line, led to the Patriots to lose four of their next six games. After beating the [Kansas City Chiefs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Kansas_City_Chiefs_season "2015 Kansas City Chiefs season") in the Divisional round, Brady faced the vaunted [Denver Broncos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Denver_Broncos_season "2015 Denver Broncos season") defense in the AFC Championship Game. The Patriots lost, 20–18 in what was Brady's last ever matchup with Peyton Manning in their rivalry, as Manning retired from the league following their Super Bowl victory over the Carolina Panthers.

### 2016: Fifth Super Bowl victory

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Tom_Brady_with_Vince_Lombardi_trophy.jpg/250px-Tom_Brady_with_Vince_Lombardi_trophy.jpg)

Belichick and Brady on stage for the Lombardi Trophy presentation after Super Bowl LI

As a result of his involvement in the [Deflategate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deflategate "Deflategate") scandal, Brady was suspended for the first four games of the [2016 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_New_England_Patriots_season "2016 New England Patriots season"). With backups [Jimmy Garoppolo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jimmy_Garoppolo "Jimmy Garoppolo") and [Jacoby Brissett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacoby_Brissett "Jacoby Brissett"), the Patriots started the year 3–1. With Brady back in Week 5, the Patriots lost just one game the rest of the season and dominated on the way to a 14–2 finish. After beating the [Houston Texans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Houston_Texans_season "2016 Houston Texans season") and the [Pittsburgh Steelers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Pittsburgh_Steelers_season "2016 Pittsburgh Steelers season") in the playoffs, the Patriots, who finished as the #3 scoring offense [^25] and the #1 scoring defense [^25] faced the [Atlanta Falcons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Atlanta_Falcons_season "2016 Atlanta Falcons season") and their top-ranked scoring offense [^25] and 27th-ranked scoring defense [^25] in [Super Bowl LI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LI "Super Bowl LI").

Early on, the Patriots' defense could not stop the high-powered Falcons offense. Brady was inconsistent, off on his throws, and threw an interception to [Robert Alford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Alford_\(American_football\) "Robert Alford (American football)") that was returned for a touchdown, the first pick-six in Brady's postseason career. By halftime, the score was 21–3. The Falcons scored again in the third quarter to make the score [28–3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/28%E2%80%933 "28–3"), a 25-point deficit. In the previous 50 Super Bowls, the largest deficit ever overcome was 10 points.[^26] The Patriots scored 31 unanswered points to win the Super Bowl 34–28 in overtime. It was the first Super Bowl to go to overtime, as well as the largest deficit overcome. Brady won his fourth Super Bowl MVP after setting a Super Bowl record with 466 passing yards. 30 Super Bowl records were broken or tied in [Super Bowl LI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LI "Super Bowl LI").[^27]

### 2017: Super Bowl loss

The Patriots started off the [2017 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_New_England_Patriots_season "2017 New England Patriots season") slow yet again, going 2–2 before going 11–1 down the stretch to win another [AFC East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AFC_East "AFC East") title and claiming the first seed in the AFC. Brady became the oldest MVP in the NFL, at 40. He also became the oldest quarterback to lead the NFL in passing with, 4,577 yards. After defeating the [Tennessee Titans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Tennessee_Titans_season "2017 Tennessee Titans season") and [Jacksonville Jaguars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Jacksonville_Jaguars_season "2017 Jacksonville Jaguars season") in the playoffs, they faced backup quarterback [Nick Foles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Foles "Nick Foles") and the high-powered [Philadelphia Eagles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Philadelphia_Eagles_season "2017 Philadelphia Eagles season") in [Super Bowl LII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LII "Super Bowl LII"). The Patriots were aiming to be the first team to win back-to-back Super Bowls since they themselves won back-to-back from 2003 to 2004. In a high-scoring shootout, Brady's attempt to lead the Patriots to another comeback victory late in the fourth quarter was stopped when Eagles defensive end [Brandon Graham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brandon_Graham "Brandon Graham") strip sacked Brady and the Eagles recovered the ball, securing a Patriots defeat, 41–33. Brady set another Super Bowl record with 505 passing yards, breaking the record he had set the year before.

### 2018: Sixth Super Bowl victory

The Patriots drew much criticism in 2018 after losing to the [Jacksonville Jaguars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Jacksonville_Jaguars_season "2018 Jacksonville Jaguars season") and the winless [Detroit Lions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Detroit_Lions_season "2018 Detroit Lions season") in the first three weeks; both teams ended below a.500 winning percentage for the season. The Patriots bounced back with a string of wins, including a [Sunday Night](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NBC_Sunday_Night_Football "NBC Sunday Night Football") thriller at home against the undefeated [Kansas City Chiefs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Kansas_City_Chiefs_season "2018 Kansas City Chiefs season"), who were led by young quarterback [Patrick Mahomes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrick_Mahomes "Patrick Mahomes"); Mahomes threw over 5,000 yards and 50 touchdowns for the season and won the NFL MVP award.

In Week 14 against the [Miami Dolphins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Miami_Dolphins_season "2018 Miami Dolphins season"), the Patriots led with 7 seconds left in the game. On the last play, Dolphins quarterback [Ryan Tannehill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryan_Tannehill "Ryan Tannehill") passed the ball to [Kenny Stills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenny_Stills "Kenny Stills"), who lateraled the ball to [DeVante Parker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DeVante_Parker "DeVante Parker"), who in turn tossed the ball to [Kenyan Drake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenyan_Drake "Kenyan Drake"). Drake then ran the remaining 53 yards for the touchdown with no time on the clock, immortalizing the play in NFL lore as the [Miracle in Miami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miracle_in_Miami "Miracle in Miami").

For the first time in almost a decade, the Patriots finished the season with more than four losses, at 11–5, but secured the AFC East for a record 10th consecutive time and the AFC's second seed, behind the 12–4 Chiefs. After handily defeating the [Los Angeles Chargers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Los_Angeles_Chargers_season "2018 Los Angeles Chargers season") by a score of 41–28 in the Divisional round, the Patriots faced Mahomes and the vaunted Chiefs offense in the AFC Championship Game. After leading 14–0 at halftime, the Patriots fought a comeback by the Chiefs and forced the game to overtime at 31–31. On the first drive of OT the Patriots quickly moved downfield through critical third down completions to [Julian Edelman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Edelman "Julian Edelman") and [Rob Gronkowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rob_Gronkowski "Rob Gronkowski"), setting up a touchdown run by [Rex Burkhead](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rex_Burkhead "Rex Burkhead"). Belichick and Brady earned their third straight trip to the Super Bowl and their ninth overall, more than any other coach/quarterback duo.

In [Super Bowl LIII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LIII "Super Bowl LIII"), the Patriots' stiff defense held the high-scoring [Los Angeles Rams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Los_Angeles_Rams_season "2018 Los Angeles Rams season") offense to three points all game, sacking quarterback [Jared Goff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jared_Goff "Jared Goff") four times, intercepting him once, and allowing only a 57.6 passer rating. The Patriots' offense started slow, with Brady throwing an early interception. They came back late in the fourth quarter, with Brady lobbing a 29-yard pass to Gronkowski, capped by a two-yard touchdown run by rookie [Sony Michel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sony_Michel "Sony Michel"). On the Patriots' next drive, kicker [Stephen Gostkowski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Gostkowski "Stephen Gostkowski") cemented the win, 13–3. It is the lowest-scoring Super Bowl ever, and Brady and Belichick's sixth and final Super Bowl win together.[^28]

### 2019: The end of the era

The Patriots started 8–0, but went 4–4 down the stretch to finish 12–4, including a stunning Week 17 loss to the [Miami Dolphins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_Miami_Dolphins_season "2019 Miami Dolphins season") at home, coached at the time by former Patriots assistant [Brian Flores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Flores "Brian Flores"), who had coached for the Patriots organization from 2004 to 2018. The loss, which was their first home loss to the Dolphins since [2008](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_New_England_Patriots_season "2008 New England Patriots season"), cost New England a first-round playoff bye, forcing them to play a wild card game for the first time since [2009](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009%E2%80%9310_NFL_playoffs "2009–10 NFL playoffs"), in which they faced the [Tennessee Titans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_Tennessee_Titans_season "2019 Tennessee Titans season"), coached by ex-Patriots linebacker and former Brady teammate [Mike Vrabel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Vrabel "Mike Vrabel"), who played for the Patriots from 2001 to 2008. The Titans shocked the Patriots 20–13 at Gillette Stadium, avenging their 2003 and 2017 Divisional round defeats at the same venue and marking the first time the Patriots had not advanced to the AFC Championship Game since 2010 and was the Titans' first win at New England since [1993](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1993_Houston_Oilers_season "1993 Houston Oilers season"), when the club was known as the [Houston Oilers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Houston_Oilers "Houston Oilers"). Brady's last pass as a Patriot was a pick-six to former teammate [Logan Ryan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logan_Ryan "Logan Ryan"), who joined the Titans in 2017 after spending his first four years with the Patriots and winning two Super Bowls (XLIX, LI).

## Aftermath

Brady joined the [Tampa Bay Buccaneers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tampa_Bay_Buccaneers "Tampa Bay Buccaneers") on March 17, 2020, separating the Belichick and Brady duo, while the Patriots signed ex- [Carolina Panthers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carolina_Panthers "Carolina Panthers") quarterback and 2015 MVP [Cam Newton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cam_Newton "Cam Newton") as their quarterback for the 2020 season. Brady and the Buccaneers won [Super Bowl LV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LV "Super Bowl LV") in his first season in Tampa Bay, while Belichick, Newton, and Brian Hoyer, finished with the Patriots' first sub-.500 season in twenty years at 7–9.[^29] [^30] Following the season, the Patriots would draft quarterback [Mac Jones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mac_Jones "Mac Jones") with the 15th-overall pick in the [2021 NFL draft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2021_NFL_draft "2021 NFL draft"). Newton, Hoyer, [Jarrett Stidham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jarrett_Stidham "Jarrett Stidham"), and Jones competed for the starting quarterback position during training camp, with Jones winning the job.[^31]

During week 4 of the 2021 season, Brady's Buccaneers visited the Patriots. Tampa Bay narrowly won [the game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2021_Tampa_Bay_Buccaneers%E2%80%93New_England_Patriots_game "2021 Tampa Bay Buccaneers–New England Patriots game") 19–17 off a missed field goal in the final seconds from Patriots kicker [Nick Folk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nick_Folk "Nick Folk").[^32] In the week leading up to the matchup, both Brady and Belichick spoke fondly of their time with one another in the press, with Brady calling Belichick a "great mentor" and Belichick calling Brady a "special player".[^33] [^34] Patriots owner [Robert Kraft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Kraft "Robert Kraft") greeted Brady outside of the locker room hours before the game, and the two exchanged warm words.[^35] Brady received a standing ovation at Gillette Stadium when he took the field for warmups, with Patriots fans chanting his name. The stadium's video screens showed a tribute video shortly before kickoff highlighting Brady's accomplishments during his 20-year tenure with the team.[^36] While fans gave Brady a warm reception before the game, the cheers turned to boos when he took the field with the Buccaneers offense.[^37]

During the game, Brady officially surpassed [Drew Brees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drew_Brees "Drew Brees") for the [most career passing yards in NFL history](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_National_Football_League_career_passing_yards_leaders "List of National Football League career passing yards leaders").[^38] With the victory over his former team, Brady became the fourth quarterback in NFL history to record a win against all 32 current teams in the league, joining [Brett Favre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brett_Favre "Brett Favre"), [Peyton Manning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peyton_Manning "Peyton Manning"), and [Drew Brees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drew_Brees "Drew Brees"). In addition, he became the sixth quarterback to have a victory against every incumbent NFL franchise after [Fran Tarkenton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fran_Tarkenton "Fran Tarkenton") and [Joe Montana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Montana "Joe Montana"), as the league only had 28 teams during the latter two's respective tenures.[^39]

Immediately after the Buccaneers victory, Brady and Belichick shared a quick embrace on the field before Brady greeted his former teammates and other members of the Patriots organization.[^40] Though fans criticized Belichick for his lack of warmth displayed towards his former quarterback, it was later reported that the two privately spoke at length in the Buccaneers locker room following the game.[^41]

Brady's return to New England became the second most-watched *Sunday Night Football* game on NBC.[^42] The broadcast also scored a 43.8 rating and 70 share in Boston, making it the city's second-biggest audience for a regular season Patriots game after [New England clinched their undefeated season against the New York Giants in 2007](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_New_England_Patriots%E2%80%93New_York_Giants_game "2007 New England Patriots–New York Giants game").[^43]

Brady announced his retirement from football on February 1, 2022, but later reversed course.[^44] He retired the next year on February 1, 2023, saying on social media that this was "for good." [^45] On September 10, Brady returned to Gillette Stadium for the first time as a non-player. In the presence of his family, he was honored by Kraft, Belichick, and the Patriots organization. In his speech, Brady declared that he was a "Patriot for life".[^46]

On January 11, 2024, Belichick and the Patriots mutually agreed to part ways.[^47] Belichick went 29–38 (0.433) in his final four seasons following Brady's departure from the Patriots. A 10-part docuseries covering the Brady–Belichick era, titled *The Dynasty*, aired on [Apple TV+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_TV%2B "Apple TV+") in early 2024.[^48]

Long Snapper [Joe Cardona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Cardona "Joe Cardona") was released by the team in April 2025. As the last survivor of the Super Bowl LIII roster, his departure was seen as a symbolic ending of the Brady-Belichick era [^49] heading into the [2025 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_New_England_Patriots_season "2025 New England Patriots season"), which was ironically a season that saw the team reach its first [Super Bowl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LX "Super Bowl LX") after the Brady-Belichick era under new head coach [Mike Vrabel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Vrabel "Mike Vrabel"), a stalwart of the first part of the era.

## The "Patriot Way"

According to many writers and analysts, the culture that Bill Belichick upheld in New England, dubbed the "Patriot Way", helped to sustain the team's success.[^50] Belichick has been known to be punctual and attentive to detail.[^50] Players such as Brady, All-Pro Safety [Devin McCourty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devin_McCourty "Devin McCourty"), and longtime Patriot [Julian Edelman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_Edelman "Julian Edelman") were known to keep the culture around New England alive. For example, Brady consistently took pay cuts despite his elite performance.[^51]

In 2017, Brady's cap value was only $14 million.[^52] Moreover, the total cash spent on Brady in 2017 was a mere $1 million.[^52] Despite such low pay, Brady played at an elite level and set several NFL records for a 40-year-old quarterback, culminating in an MVP award and a record-breaking 505 passing yards in a losing effort in [Super Bowl LII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LII "Super Bowl LII"). When asked why he accepted pay cuts and a salary below market value, Brady replied, "I think the thing I've always felt for me in my life, winning has been a priority. And my wife \[model [Gisele Bündchen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gisele_B%C3%BCndchen "Gisele Bündchen")\] makes a lot of money. I'm a little smarter than you think... Actually, it's a salary cap. You can only spend so much and the more that one guy gets is less for others. And for a competitive advantage standpoint, I like to get a lot of good players around me." [^53] Brady's willingness to take pay cuts allowed the Patriots to spend that money elsewhere on additional skill players. A famous example of this was in the signing of Hall of Fame wide receiver [Randy Moss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randy_Moss "Randy Moss") in 2007. According to Patriots executives, Brady restructured his contract to allow the Patriots to acquire the cap space needed to sign Moss. Moss went on to set the NFL single-season receiving touchdown record.

Belichick's disciplinarianism was shown with running back [Jonas Gray](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonas_Gray "Jonas Gray"). In Week 11 of the 2014 season against the Indianapolis Colts, Gray rushed for 201 yards and scored four touchdowns, a franchise record. But he was late to practice the next week, something Belichick is known not to tolerate. Gray was active in only five more games for the Patriots, and was released less than a year later.[^50]

## Controversies

**Spygate** was an incident during the 2007 NFL season, when the Patriots were disciplined by the league for videotaping [New York Jets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Jets "New York Jets") ' defensive coaches' signals from an unauthorized location. Videotaping opposing coaches is not illegal in the NFL [de jure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De_jure "De jure"), but there are designated areas allowed by the league to do such taping. Because the Patriots were instead videotaping the Jets' coaches from their own sideline, NFL Commissioner [Roger Goodell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roger_Goodell "Roger Goodell") deemed it to be in violation of league rules, stating that the act represented a calculated and deliberate attempt to avoid long-standing rules designed to encourage fair play and promote honest competition.

After an investigation, the NFL fined Belichick $500,000 (the maximum allowed by the league and the largest fine ever imposed on a coach in league history) for his role in the incident, fined the Patriots $250,000, and docked the team their first-round selection in the 2008 NFL Draft. After the original incident, several other allegations were made against the Patriots filming opposing teams.[^54]

**Deflategate** was a controversy involving the allegation that Brady ordered the deliberate deflating of footballs used in the Patriots' victory against the [Indianapolis Colts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indianapolis_Colts "Indianapolis Colts") in the 2014–15 AFC Championship Game. The controversy resulted in Brady being suspended for four games, the Patriots being fined $1 million and losing two draft picks, and saw the NFL later change the procedure for monitoring football pressure.[^55]

For his alleged part in the scandal, Brady was originally suspended by the league for four games of the 2015 regular season, but a federal judge later vacated his suspension and allowed Brady to resume his playing duties for the entirety of the 2015 season.[^56] However, the 2nd U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals reinstated Brady's four-game suspension, which became effective for the 2016 regular season.[^57] After serving his suspension, the Patriots would go on to win that season's [Super Bowl LI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_LI "Super Bowl LI").
