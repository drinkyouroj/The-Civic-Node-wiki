In early April 2026, DDR5 retail prices plunged more than 30% from recent highs in Chinese retail markets, leaving vendors with large stockpiles.

Pricing data:
- One wholesaler reported a mainstream 16GB DDR5 module dropped over $14.50 in a single day
- Further declines of $5.80-$7.25 followed in subsequent days
- 16GB DDR5 sticks that previously sold for 1,600-1,800 RMB ($232-$261) fell to around 1,200 RMB ($174)
- Shenzhen's Huaqiangbei Electronics Market: 32GB modules dropped from near RMB 3,000 to as low as RMB 1,950 in fire-sale pricing

Chinese memory vendors found themselves stuck with huge memory stockpiles with nowhere to offload them at inflated prices. Traders and resellers that bought DDR5 in bulk during the run-up struggled to clear inventory, with some sellers cutting prices quickly.

Cause of the collapse: The sharp correction was triggered by a combination of market saturation, speculative sell-offs, and Google's TurboQuant announcement (March 24, 2026). TurboQuant demonstrated a 6x reduction in LLM memory requirements, which undermined the demand projections justifying the stockpiling.

VideoCardz reported that Chinese memory stockpilers "started to panic as falling prices leave them stuck with modules."

Despite retail price drops, contract prices between manufacturers and OEMs remained stable — the correction was concentrated in the speculative retail/reseller layer.
