# The Letter That Moved a Market

Analysis of OpenAI's non-binding letter of intent for DRAM supply and its market consequences.

## LOI Terms and Structure

The LOI was non-binding with no legal obligation to purchase. Signed in October 2025 between Sam Altman and Samsung/SK Hynix for 900,000 DRAM wafer starts per month, representing approximately 40% of global DRAM output. Valued at $71.3 billion over four years.

## Supplier Impact

- Samsung: Ramped HBM production toward 250,000 wafers monthly (47% capacity increase)
- SK Hynix: Expanded to 620,000 DRAM wafers monthly, with significant share for HBM conversion
- Together: Reallocated up to 40% of advanced wafer capacity to AI-specific memory

## Market Impact

DRAM contract prices rose 171% year over year by the third quarter of 2025. A 64GB DDR5 kit jumped from $180 (May) to $710 (December) -- a 294% increase. Quote validity windows compressed from 30-90 days to as little as 7 days. Some distributors reported hourly spot pricing.

## Demand Signal Collapse (March 2026)

- Oracle/OpenAI scrapped Stargate expansion (March 6)
- OpenAI shut down Sora platform (March 24)
- Google released TurboQuant compression algorithm (March 25)
- Samsung shifted 80,000 wafers back to DDR5 due to superior margins
