### Flagged Claims

#### Claim #A: "Neither supplier knew about the other's deal"
**Article says:** "[Neither supplier knew about the other's deal.](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/01/openai-ropes-in-samsung-sk-hynix-to-source-memory-chips-for-stargate/)"
**Source says:** TechCrunch describes a **joint signing ceremony** in Seoul between "OpenAI CEO Sam Altman, South Korea's president Lee Jae-myung, Samsung Electronics' executive chairman Jay Y. Lee, and SK chairman Chey Tae-won." All parties were in the same room.
**Discrepancy:** The TechCrunch article does not say the suppliers were unaware of each other's deals. It reports the opposite — a joint meeting. The "neither knew" claim originates from MLID's investigative reporting about the earlier negotiation phase, not from TechCrunch.
**Recommendation:** This is a factual claim linked to a source that contradicts it. Three options: (1) Remove the claim entirely — the joint signing ceremony complicates the "secret deal" narrative. (2) Reframe: "The deals were negotiated in parallel" or "The scale of the combined commitment — 40% of global capacity — was not apparent until both were announced" and link to the TrendForce article which covers the combined scale. (3) If you want to keep the "neither knew" claim, it needs a source that actually reports this — the only one we have is MLID (Tier 4), which cannot be linked in the published article per your source authority rules. **This may require dropping or reframing the claim.**

#### Claim #B: TBPN "58,000 YouTube subscribers" and "low hundreds of millions"
**Article says:** "had just [acquired a podcast](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/02/openai-acquires-tbpn-the-buzzy-founder-led-business-talk-show/) with 58,000 YouTube subscribers for a reported sum in the low hundreds of millions"
**Source says:** TechCrunch reports TBPN is "on track to pull in more than $30 million this year" (citing WSJ). The article does **not** contain "58,000 YouTube subscribers" or "low hundreds of millions."
**Discrepancy:** Two facts in this sentence are not in the linked source. The subscriber count doesn't appear in any Tier 1-2 source I've checked. The "low hundreds of millions" figure was reported by the Financial Times, not TechCrunch.
**Recommendation:** Drop the "58,000 YouTube subscribers" detail (it's unsourced color from the Big A transcript). For the acquisition price, either link to the FT reporting directly or soften to "for a reported nine-figure sum" and keep the TechCrunch link, which covers the acquisition itself without the price.
