### How arresting Don Lemon exposed MAGA's constitutional cosplay

It was December, 2024. We were at my sister’s house. A family member chuckled at my prediction about mass deportations sweeping up citizens. Not nervous laughter. The confident, dismissive kind that says you’re being hysterical.

“Your constitutional rights won’t matter when ICE decides you look or sound wrong,” I said to him.

He promised me that couldn’t happen. “It’s unconstitutional.”

That word again. Like a magic spell.

I’m writing this because federal agents arrested Don Lemon for doing his job. A 30-year journalist taken into custody by the FBI and Homeland Security Investigations for the crime of watching a protest. Minnesota Chief U.S. District Judge Patrick Schiltz reviewed the evidence and wrote that Lemon and his producer were “not protesters at all” and “there is no evidence that those two engaged in any criminal behavior.” [^1]

A federal magistrate judge rejected the charges. Twice. No evidence of wrongdoing.

Attorney General Pam Bondi was reportedly “enraged” by judicial decisions that followed the law. So she went judge shopping, empaneled a grand jury, and had federal agents arrest Lemon at a Beverly Hills hotel while he was covering the Grammy Awards. The White House celebrated by posting a meme with chains emoji and the caption “When life gives you lemons...” [^2]

Here’s what Lemon was covering: protesters demanding accountability at a church where one of the pastors runs ICE’s local gestapo. David Easterwood is both a pastor at Cities Church in St. Paul and the acting director of the ICE St. Paul field office overseeing Operation Metro Surge.[^3] The same operation that deployed 3,000 armed, masked federal agents to Minnesota. The same operation where Renee Good and Alex Pretti were shot and killed for exercising their constitutional rights.

The Constitution didn’t save them. And it didn’t save Don Lemon.

## The Glitch: When Your Pastor Runs the Local Gestapo

David Easterwood appeared on C-SPAN in October standing next to DHS Secretary Kristi Noem, talking about how “proud” he was of leading his ICE team.[^4] He’s listed on the Cities Church website as a pastor.[^5] Same person. Dual roles.

In court filings dated January 5, Easterwood defended ICE tactics in Minnesota. He wrote that swapping license plates, spraying protesters with chemical irritants, and using flash-bang grenades were necessary tools to protect against “violent attacks.” He testified under oath that he was unaware of agents “knowingly targeting or retaliating against peaceful protesters.” [^6]

Five days later, ICE agent Jonathan Ross shot Renee Good three times while she sat in her vehicle. Video shows her telling Ross, “That’s fine, dude. I’m not mad at you” seconds before he fired.[^7] She was a 37-year-old mother and U.S. citizen whose crime was observing federal agents in her neighborhood. Exercising her First Amendment right.

Seventeen days later, federal agents shot and killed Alex Pretti, a 37-year-old legal gun owner who was also observing ICE operations. DHS claimed he “brandished” a weapon and “attacked” officers.[^8] Video evidence and eyewitness accounts show nothing of the sort. He was watching law enforcement while exercising his Second Amendment right to carry a firearm.

This is the context protesters brought to Easterwood’s church on January 18. Not random agitators. People demanding that a pastor who oversees an operation killing U.S. citizens resign from his church position. People chanting “Justice for Renee Good” in the sanctuary where the man responsible for her death teaches about loving your neighbor.

Don Lemon was there with a camera and a microphone. He posted video to Instagram showing his interviews with churchgoers and protesters. The Washington Post reviewed the footage: 45 minutes inside the church, conversations with four parishioners and five protesters, mostly calm exchanges. Lemon didn’t participate in the chants disrupting the service. A pastor asked him to leave. Seven minutes later, he walked out.[^9]

Throughout the video, Lemon repeatedly identified himself as a journalist. “We’re not part of the activists, but we’re here just reporting on them,” he says on camera. “I’m not here as an activist. I’m here as a journalist.” [^10]

Minnesota Chief Judge Patrick Schiltz watched the same footage. His conclusion: Lemon and his producer were “not protesters at all” and “there is no evidence that those two engaged in any criminal behavior or conspiracy to commit a crime.”

A federal magistrate judge agreed. Rejected the charges. No probable cause.[^11]

Pam Bondi didn’t care. She went around the judge, empaneled a grand jury, and indicted Lemon anyway on charges of conspiracy to deprive others of their civil rights and violation of the FACE Act by “interfering by force” with churchgoers’ First Amendment rights.[^12]

Read that again. A journalist is charged with violating the First Amendment rights of people attending a church where the pastor runs the operation that killed two people for exercising their First Amendment and Second Amendment rights.

## The Source Code: How MAGA’s Principles Died in Minnesota

The Second Amendment hypocrisy deserves an autopsy. MAGA spent years building an entire political identity around gun rights as protection against tyranny. The narrative went like this: when government agents come for you, the armed citizen stands as the final check on power. “Molon labe.” “From my cold, dead hands.” All that performative defiance.

Alex Pretti was that armed citizen. Legal gun owner. Observing federal agents. Not threatening anyone. Just watching, armed, as the Founders supposedly intended.

Federal agents shot him. Killed him.

MAGA’s response? Defending the agents. Kristi Noem claimed Pretti “brandished” his weapon and “attacked” officers despite video evidence showing nothing of the sort. The same crowd that spent decades screaming about Ruby Ridge and Waco suddenly decided that actually, when federal agents kill armed citizens observing law enforcement, that’s fine.

The principle evaporated the moment it became inconvenient.

Here’s the pattern: MAGA claims to worship constitutional rights but only when exercised by the right people. Renee Good exercising her First Amendment right to observe government action? Shot three times, labeled a “domestic terrorist.” [^13] Alex Pretti exercising his Second Amendment right to carry a firearm? Shot and killed, accused of attacking agents. Don Lemon exercising his First Amendment right to report on a protest? Arrested, charged with violating others’ rights, mocked by the White House.

Meanwhile, David Easterwood exercises his right to run ICE operations that kill U.S. citizens while moonlighting as a pastor teaching about Jesus. That dual role gets federal protection. That conflict of interest gets Attorney General Pam Bondi personally directing arrests of journalists who dare to question it.

The Fourth Amendment took similar hits. By January 28, Minnesota Chief U.S. District Judge Patrick Schiltz found that ICE had violated at least 96 court orders in Minnesota since January 1 alone.[^14] Ninety-six times in 28 days that federal agents looked at a judge’s order and decided it didn’t apply to them. Over three violations per day. That’s not enforcement getting overzealous. That’s systematic contempt for judicial authority.

An internal ICE memo dated May 12, 2025, claims agents can forcibly enter homes using only administrative warrants issued by immigration officers themselves, not judges.[^15] On January 11, federal agents broke into a Liberian immigrant’s home with a battering ram despite having only this administrative paperwork. He’d been checking in regularly with immigration authorities for years. U.S. District Judge Jeffrey Bryan ruled the forced entry was a warrantless search violating the Fourth Amendment.[^16]

One ruling. Then 95 more violations the same month.

U.S. citizen Mubashir Khalif Hussen, 20 years old, was walking to lunch on December 10 when masked ICE agents stopped him. “I’m a citizen. I’m a citizen,” he repeated. The agents refused to look at his ID. They put him in an SUV, drove him to a facility, shackled him, took his fingerprints, and only released him after he showed a photo of his passport card.[^17]

His crime? Walking while Somali in Minneapolis.

## The Upgrade: When Polling Data Becomes a Political Funeral

Americans have turned violently against ICE, and the numbers tell the story MAGA wants to bury.

February 2025, right after Trump’s inauguration: ICE had a +16 net approval rating according to YouGov polling. By January 2026: -14. A 30-point collapse in under a year.[^18]

Data for Progress found that 51% of voters now view ICE unfavorably versus 42% favorably.[^19] The New York Times/Siena poll put ICE’s disapproval at 63%.[^20] More devastating: 61% of Americans say ICE’s tactics have “gone too far.” Only 11% think they haven’t gone far enough.[^21] That’s a 50-point gap.

Fox News found that 59% of voters view ICE as “too aggressive,” up 10 points just since July 2025.[^22] Support for abolishing ICE entirely has doubled since 2018, jumping from 18% to 42%.[^23] Among Democrats, it’s now 69%. That’s not a fringe position anymore. That’s consensus.

After Alex Pretti’s killing, YouGov found that more Americans support abolishing ICE than oppose it.[^24] First time that’s ever happened in polling. The agency has lost its legitimacy in the eyes of the American public.

Why? Because people are watching the videos. They’re seeing U.S. citizens detained for hours because they “don’t have the same accent.” They’re watching peaceful protesters get pepper-sprayed and arrested. They’re seeing Renee Good tell an agent she’s not mad at him seconds before he shoots her three times. They’re watching Alex Pretti get killed for legally carrying a firearm. They’re seeing federal agents arrest a journalist for documenting a protest at a church where the pastor runs ICE operations.

The abstract idea of “strict immigration enforcement” polls well. The concrete reality of masked federal agents breaking into homes with battering rams, shooting mothers in their cars, killing legal gun owners, racially profiling American citizens, and arresting journalists for watching does not.

CBS News/YouGov polling shows 52% of Americans believe ICE is making communities less safe. Not more safe. Less. The thing ICE supposedly does actually achieves the opposite effect in the eyes of a majority of Americans.

The kicker: Americans now approve of protests against ICE more than they approve of ICE itself. YouGov found 44% approve of anti-ICE protests versus 42% who disapprove. Compare that to ICE’s 39% approval and 52% disapproval. The protesters are more popular than the agency. The people chanting “Justice for Renee Good” in that church have more public support than the pastor who runs the operation that killed her.

Trump threatened to sue the Times and Siena over their polling showing his approval at 34%. He called it “fake and fraudulent” and suggested polling should be “virtually, a criminal offense.” [^25] That’s the response of someone who knows he’s losing the narrative badly.

## My Debug: When Comfortable Delusions Meet Battering Rams

That conversation at my sister’s house keeps replaying. Not because I enjoy being right. Because I’m furious that being right meant Renee Good and Alex Pretti had to die and Don Lemon had to get arrested for people to pay attention.

I’ve spent my life watching people ignore warnings until crisis forces recognition. The reflexive dismissal of uncomfortable truths. The preference for comforting myths over difficult realities. The shocked surprise when the predicted disaster actually materializes.

This isn’t about immigration policy. This is about whether we’re a nation of laws or a nation of gestapo tactics wrapped in flag-waving rhetoric. Classical liberalism (the actual Enlightenment philosophy that built this country) rests on the idea that individual rights constrain government power. Authorities need warrants. Citizens can protest. Gun owners can carry legally without being murdered for it. Journalists can document government action without being arrested. “Because I said so” isn’t enough justification for state violence.

Trump’s ICE has violated every one of those principles in Minnesota. First Amendment? Renee Good killed for observing agents. Don Lemon arrested for documenting a protest. Second Amendment? Alex Pretti killed for legally carrying a firearm. Fourth Amendment? Ninety-six court order violations in 28 days. Tenth Amendment? Federal agents occupying a state against the wishes of its government.[^26]

The Constitution didn’t protect anyone. The piece of paper my family member cited as his shield became evidence at murder scenes and the basis for federal charges against a journalist.

That family member is Hispanic. He thought his ethnicity wouldn’t matter because he’s a citizen. He thought the Constitution would protect him from exactly what’s happening in Minnesota right now: warrantless home invasions, racial profiling, U.S. citizens detained for hours because an ICE agent said “I can hear you don’t have the same accent as me.”

MAGA has demonstrated with absolute clarity that they have no principles whatsoever. They claim to worship the Constitution while celebrating its demolition. They scream about government tyranny while cheering jackbooted thugs kicking down doors. They fetishize the Second Amendment until a legal gun owner gets killed by federal agents, then suddenly due process is optional. They wave flags while the values those flags represent burn. They arrest journalists for watching their pastor run a gestapo operation, then post memes mocking the arrest.

They are not good-faith participants in democracy. They are obstacles to it.

Bruce Springsteen dedicated “The Promised Land” to Renee Good at a New Jersey concert: “If you believe in the power of law and that no one stands above it, if you stand against heavily-armed masked federal troops invading an American city, using gestapo tactics against our fellow citizens, if you believe you don’t deserve to be murdered for exercising your American right to protest, then send a message to this president, as the mayor of the city said: ICE should get the fuck out of Minneapolis.” [^27]

He’s right. But messages alone won’t stop this.

We need legal resistance. Lawsuits like Minnesota’s challenging the constitutional violations. We need political resistance. Voting out every representative who funds this machinery, pressuring elected officials to act. We need documentation. Recording every interaction, preserving evidence, building the case. We need economic pressure. Boycotts, strikes, making the occupation too expensive to maintain.

Civil disobedience has its place, but understand the stakes clearly: this administration has proven it will shoot protesters and arrest journalists. Renee Good is dead. Alex Pretti is dead. Don Lemon is facing federal charges. The calculus of risk has fundamentally changed when federal agents are firing into crowds and vehicles, then arresting the people who document it.

The Constitution couldn’t save Renee Good or Alex Pretti or Don Lemon. But maybe their cases will finally wake up the comfortable people who thought fascism couldn’t happen here. It is happening. In Minnesota. In real time. With video evidence. With federal agents arresting journalists for watching a pastor who runs ICE operations defend his dual role from the pulpit.

I told them this would happen. They laughed. Now two Americans are dead, a journalist is facing federal charges, and constitutional rights are suggestions that federal agents ignore whenever convenient.

Still think it can’t happen? Ask Renee Good’s three children. Ask Alex Pretti’s family. Ask Don Lemon’s lawyer. Ask Mubashir Hussen, who spent hours shackled for walking while Somali. Ask anyone in Minnesota whether the Constitution protected them.

The document didn’t save anyone. People willing to resist saved what could be saved. Filing lawsuits, protesting despite the danger, documenting the atrocities, refusing cooperation with tyranny.

That’s the only thing that’s ever stopped this pattern. Not *hoping* the rules will protect us; *fighting* to make sure they do.

*What will it take for you to stop waiting for the Constitution to save you and start defending it yourself? And when federal agents come to your neighborhood arresting journalists for watching their operations, will you remember that someone tried to warn you?*
