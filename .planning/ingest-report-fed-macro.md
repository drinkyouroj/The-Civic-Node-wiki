2. **PWBM is the strongest quantitative anchor**: $22K lifetime household loss, -6% long-run GDP, 2x more damaging than a corporate tax increase. These are the numbers to cite when defending the economic magnitude of tariff policy.

3. **Government shutdown as data weaponization**: The BLS shutdown didn't just delay data — the September jobs report was likely in final draft form. This is better understood as information control than operational disruption.

4. **Energy grants cancellation tracks political lines exactly**: NOTUS methodology (hundreds of projects) makes this the most rigorous evidence that the shutdown was used as active political leverage — not just fiscal constraint.

5. **M2 contraction during inflation**: M2 was negative YoY throughout 2023 while inflation remained elevated — the strongest single piece of evidence that 2025 inflation is supply-shock driven, not monetary.

6. **Michigan as leading indicator**: Largest unemployment rate increase of any state (4.2%→5.5%), second-highest in nation. Tariff impacts hitting Michigan first and hardest because of auto supply chain exposure.
