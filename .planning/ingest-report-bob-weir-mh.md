## Notable Themes Surfaced

- **Support roles as invisible architecture**: Weir's rhythm guitar work — deliberately subordinate to Garcia's lead — turns out to be the structural innovation that made the Dead's sound possible. The "backstage" contribution as the analytically interesting story.
- **Cross-domain learning as the source of originality**: Weir's breakthrough came from studying jazz piano (McCoy Tyner), not other guitarists. Appears as a pattern in the Weir cluster.
- **Perpetual crisis governance as intentional public health harm**: The Simply Put Psych essay provides a five-mechanism framework for why Trump-era governance is neurobiologically corrosive — not just politically contentious.
- **DEA regulatory rigidity**: The ADHD shortage cluster now has a clean mechanistic account of how the DEA's quota system creates harm — the clearest regulatory-weaponization story in the wiki.
- **Mental health data as the most exploitable data class**: BetterHelp's conduct (using therapy intake data for Facebook lookalike audiences) is the starkest case of intimate data monetization in the wiki.
