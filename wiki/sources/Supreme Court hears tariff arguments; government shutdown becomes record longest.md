## Summary
NBC News live blog from November 5, 2025 covering two major stories: the Supreme Court hearing oral arguments on Trump's tariffs (justices across ideological spectrum skeptical), and the government shutdown hitting day 36 — the longest in US history, beating Trump's own first-term record of 35 days. Also covers post-election analysis showing Democratic sweep.

## Key Points
- Day 36: government shutdown breaks record for longest ever; beats 35-day Trump first-term record; shutdown began Oct. 1, 2025
- SNAP benefits: Agriculture Dept. told federal court beneficiaries would receive 65% of normal monthly rate (up from 50% initially estimated)
- Supreme Court tariff arguments: justices across ideological spectrum appeared skeptical of Trump's use of IEEPA emergency law to impose sweeping tariffs
- Sen. Ted Cruz: called 2025 election results "a disaster" and "an electoral blowout"; "the left showed up in big numbers and common sense conservatives did not"
- Trump told Fox News he'd eliminate the filibuster if he could; pressed Senate Republicans on it at White House breakfast
- Trump said he expected New Jersey to "do better" but chalked NJ, VA, and NY losses up to being "pretty Democrat states"
- Democrats from Caribbean strikes briefing demanded full Senate briefing — concern about legal justification for strikes against alleged drug smugglers

## Newsletter Angles
- The shutdown breaking Trump's own record is a clean symmetry: Trump shut the government down during his first term for 35 days; now his second-term shutdown beats it. The administration that promised to end chaos is producing record-setting chaos
- Supreme Court skepticism on tariffs during same week as Democratic wave election creates a convergence: Trump's signature economic move may be legally invalidated the same cycle his party is losing on economic grounds
