## Summary
Reports the breakdown of the joint federal-state investigation into Good's killing after the FBI revoked the Minnesota Bureau of Criminal Apprehension's access to evidence — the day after the shooting. Minnesota officials said independent investigation would be "extremely difficult, if not impossible."

## Key Points
- FBI revoked MN BCA's access to evidence one day after the shooting
- Minnesota public safety commissioner: continuing without federal cooperation "extremely difficult, if not impossible"
- This occurred after an initial agreement for a joint investigation
- Represents the federal government actively blocking state accountability for federal conduct
