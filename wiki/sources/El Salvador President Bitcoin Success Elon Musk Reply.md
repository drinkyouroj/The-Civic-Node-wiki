## Summary

Coverage of Nayib Bukele's announcement in December 2024 that El Salvador's Bitcoin holdings had surpassed $600 million in valuation, coinciding with Bitcoin breaking $100,000 for the first time. Elon Musk replied "Impressive" to Bukele's posts. The article contextualizes Bitcoin's rally as driven by Trump's electoral win and pro-crypto SEC appointment.

## Key Points

- El Salvador's Bitcoin holdings valued at ~$603M in December 2024; initial investment ~$269.7M; unrealized profit ~$333M.
- Bitcoin crossed $100,000 for the first time; at time of publication was at $103,465.
- Elon Musk replied "Impressive" to Bukele's posts; Bukele shared a meme in response.
- Bitcoin rally attributed to Trump election win and appointment of Paul Atkins (crypto-friendly) as SEC chair nominee.
- Bitcoin had risen ~45% since Trump's election victory, driven by Bitcoin ETF inflows.
- El Salvador adopted Bitcoin as legal tender in mid-2021 at ~$36,000.

## Newsletter Angles

- The Bukele-Musk interaction is a perfect emblem of the convergence between crypto maximalism and Trump-era political power — two outsider power centers validating each other.
- El Salvador's unrealized $333M profit at the time undercuts the "failed experiment" narrative — the financial bet was working even as adoption wasn't.
- Bitcoin's rally as proxy for political expectations: the market priced in a permissive regulatory environment under Trump before any rules changed.
