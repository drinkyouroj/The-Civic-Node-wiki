## Summary

On Easter Sunday (April 5, 2026), Trump issued an expletive-laden Truth Social ultimatum threatening to strike Iran's power plants and bridges on Tuesday, April 8 at 8 PM ET, unless the Strait of Hormuz is reopened. The threat follows over five weeks of US-Israeli strikes on Iran that began February 28, 2026, after which Iran closed the Strait — a chokepoint carrying ~20% of global oil. Gas prices have risen from under $3 to $4.11/gallon since strikes began. Trump simultaneously claimed Iran is willing to negotiate while threatening total destruction.

## Key Points

- Trump set a precise deadline: **Tuesday April 8, 8 PM ET** — strike Iran's power plants and bridges if Strait not reopened.
- US-Israel war on Iran began **February 28, 2026**; Iran closed Strait of Hormuz in retaliation.
- **13 US service members killed**, hundreds wounded as of April 5.
- A **48-hour rescue operation** extracted two US pilots from inside Iran; one "seriously wounded Colonel."
- US gasoline hit **$4.11/gallon**, up from under $3 at war start — a ~37% spike in ~5 weeks.
- Trump told Fox News Iran is negotiating; told ABC "we're blowing up the whole country" if no deal.
- Trump threatened to **"take" Iran's oil** as an alternative to strikes.
- Prior threat: targeting **desalination plants**, which international law experts said may violate humanitarian law.
- Iran's IRGC warned it would intensify attacks on **US economic interests in the Gulf** if civilian targets hit again.
- **Marjorie Taylor Greene** — formerly Trump's most vocal defender, recently resigned — called his rhetoric "madness."
- **Tim Kaine** (D-VA, Armed Services): language "embarrassing and juvenile," raises risk for US service members.
- **Jake Auchincloss** (D-MA, Marine vet): Iran's Strait control is more strategic than its nuclear program; "strategically, this war has been a failure."
- **Rick Crawford** (R-AR, House Intel Chair): defended Trump — "He has a lot of backbone."
