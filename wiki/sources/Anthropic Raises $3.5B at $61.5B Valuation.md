## Summary
Anthropic closed a $3.5B funding round led by Lightspeed Venture Partners, reaching a $61.5B valuation. The round positions Anthropic as the clear #2 to OpenAI (valued at $157B at time of writing, with a rumored $340B round pending) and funds AI safety research, compute expansion, mechanistic interpretability, and international expansion into Asia and Europe.

## Key Points
- $3.5B round led by Lightspeed ($1B); other investors include Bessemer, Cisco, D1 Capital, Fidelity, General Catalyst, Jane Street, Menlo, Salesforce Ventures
- Valuation: $61.5B vs OpenAI's $157B (rumored $340B); Google DeepMind; Meta; Mistral ($6B)
- Funds earmarked for: next-gen AI systems, compute, mechanistic interpretability/alignment research, Asia/Europe expansion
- Claude 3.7 Sonnet introduced "hybrid reasoning" — toggling between fast and deliberative thinking modes
- Claude Code also launched in this period
- Amazon is both a major Anthropic investor and building competing models (Nova project)
- Meta launching standalone AI chatbot to challenge Claude directly

## Newsletter Angles
- Amazon's dual role (major investor + competitor building Nova) is a structural conflict that deserves scrutiny — how does that relationship actually work?
- The "safety" branding vs. scale race: Anthropic's pitch is safety-first, but a $61.5B valuation requires growth that creates its own pressures. The fundraising race and the safety mission are in tension.
- International expansion into Asia/Europe at the same time Anthropic is fighting the US DoD blacklisting creates an interesting geopolitical narrative: expelled from one government's supply chain, courted by others.
