## Summary

California Governor's office press release documenting the state's independent hospice fraud enforcement record: 280+ licenses revoked, 300+ under investigation, 284 criminals arrested, with a moratorium on new hospice licenses in place since 2021. The release frames this as a direct rebuttal to Trump administration CMS attacks on California's enforcement posture — and notes that the federal government has put its own hospice fraud crackdown "on hold."

## Key Points

- 280+ hospice licenses revoked by California CDPH in the past two years
- ~300 additional hospices currently being evaluated for revocation
- 284 criminal defendants arrested; 109 individuals charged with hospice-related offenses; 24 civil cases filed
- Moratorium on new hospice licenses signed 2021 (SB 664), extended 2024 (AB 177) — the moratorium predates the Trump administration by years
- California operates a multi-agency Hospice Fraud Task Force (CDPH, DHCS, DSS, DOJ's DMFEA)
- Axios reported Trump admin put federal hospice crackdown "on hold" — resetting efforts to root out fraud in an industry receiving $25B+ annually from Medicare
- California DHCS uses AI-powered fraud detection; requires verified authorization forms before any hospice payments; implementing prior authorization requirements
- Key jurisdictional note: Medicare is federally administered (CMS); Medi-Cal is state-administered. California's enforcement covers Medi-Cal. Federal officials attacking CA on Medicare fraud are attacking something outside California's jurisdiction

## Newsletter Angles

- **The inversion story**: The CMS narrative frames California as permissive and fraud-ridden. The actual record shows California built enforcement infrastructure years before federal pressure — and the federal government is now dismantling its own oversight. This is a clean reversal of the official narrative.
- **Jurisdiction shell game**: CMS officials (including Oz) attack California over Medicare fraud — but Medicare is a federal program. California's Medi-Cal enforcement is the only enforcement actually within the state's jurisdiction. The attacks conflate two different programs to generate political heat.
- **The $25B question**: Federal hospice fraud exceeds $25B annually in Medicare payments. The Trump admin put its crackdown "on hold." That number, next to the enforcement suspension, is the story.
- **State power as institutional resistance**: California's enforcement record demonstrates that states with institutional infrastructure (legislation, multi-agency task forces, AI fraud detection) can operate independently of federal policy — and that this independence becomes politically threatening to federal actors trying to use enforcement as a cudgel.

## Notes

**Source bias**: This is a government press release from the Governor's office. It is advocacy, not neutral reporting. All statistics should be treated as official state figures, not independently verified.

**What it doesn't address**: The release does not engage with the specific hospice fraud cases federal officials have highlighted (e.g., the "Russian Armenian mafia" framing from Oz), nor does it provide Medicare fraud data (outside California's jurisdiction). The framing is entirely defensive.

**What it does establish**: The moratorium and task force predate the Trump administration by years. The enforcement record (280+ revocations, 284 arrests) is documented state action, not posturing.
