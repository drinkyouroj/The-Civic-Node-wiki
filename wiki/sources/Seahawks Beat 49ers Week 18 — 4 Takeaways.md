## Summary

Fox Sports analysis of the Seahawks' 13-3 Week 18 win over the 49ers to clinch the NFC's #1 seed. Seattle's first 14-win season in franchise history. Sam Darnold played mistake-free football after a rocky stretch; the running game dominated; the defense held McCaffrey to 23 rushing yards.

## Key Points

- Seahawks clinched first 14-win season in franchise history; 4th time as NFC's #1 seed (all three previous times led to Super Bowl appearances)
- First regular-season finale in NFL history with both teams entering on 6-game winning streaks (Seahawks and 49ers)
- Darnold: 20/26, 198 yards, 0 TDs, 0 turnovers — efficient, no mistakes (critical after 20 turnovers on the season)
- Kenneth Walker III: 97 yards; Zach Charbonnet: 74 yards + TD — combined 180 rushing yards vs. a defense allowing 103/game
- Defense held McCaffrey to 23 rushing yards and 57 scrimmage yards (he entered with 2,069 scrimmage yards and 17 TDs)
- LB Drake Thomas intercepted Brock Purdy in the red zone — critical 4th-quarter stop

## Newsletter Angles

- Darnold's redemption narrative: the same player who collapsed in Week 18 with the Vikings in 2024 (same situation, same stakes) delivered a clean performance for Seattle in 2025
- Run game as scheme expression: Macdonald's defense-first philosophy creates a game-flow environment where the run game can thrive — the offense became more efficient when the defense gave it short fields
