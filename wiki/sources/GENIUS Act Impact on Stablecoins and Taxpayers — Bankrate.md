## Summary

Bankrate consumer-finance perspective on the GENIUS Act, including a focused analysis from Georgetown law professor Adam Levitin arguing the GENIUS Act's bankruptcy priority provisions set up a future government bailout of the stablecoin sector. The piece explains how stablecoins work, their role as crypto reserve currency, and the risks the GENIUS Act doesn't resolve.

## Key Points

- Tether: third largest crypto by market cap (after Bitcoin and Ethereum); holds T-bills, Bitcoin, gold — not just cash.
- Stablecoins function as crypto's "reserve currency" — proceeds of crypto sales land in stablecoins; stablecoins enable precise pricing.
- GENIUS Act: limits issuance to permitted parties; sets reserve/disclosure requirements; establishes AML procedures; gives stablecoin owners higher bankruptcy priority.
- Bank of America developing a dollar-pegged stablecoin; Walmart and Amazon may issue stablecoins.
- **Bailout argument** (Georgetown's Adam Levitin): GENIUS Act rewrites established bankruptcy law; stablecoin holders given priority over administrative claims (lawyers, professionals) in bankruptcy — but lawyers won't work without guaranteed payment, making orderly wind-downs impossible.
- A broken stablecoin peg (like TerraUSD 2022) creates a situation where GENIUS Act promises safety but "cannot deliver on that promise" — government then forced to provide safety on its own dime.
- "It sets up a bailout" — crypto industry that donated heavily in 2024 is "primed for a bailout when things go wrong."
- TerraUSD 2022 collapse ($40B+ wiped out) as cautionary example.

## Newsletter Angles

- The bailout argument is the most significant unaddressed risk in the GENIUS Act. Levitin's analysis: insolvency priority reform + implicit government backing promise = future TBTF (too big to fail) crypto.
- Bank of America, Walmart, Amazon entering stablecoin issuance is a signal that regulatory clarity will drive massive corporate adoption — and massive government dependency.
- The "stablecoins as reserve currency of crypto" framing explains their systemic importance: if stablecoins fail, the entire crypto market collapses.
