## Summary

Baltimore Beatdown piece from the day the Ravens announced Macdonald as defensive coordinator (February 2022), transcribing the press conference where Macdonald and HC John Harbaugh laid out the philosophy behind the hire. Key insight: Macdonald explicitly redefines "aggression" from blitz-volume to information-warfare — keeping defenses off-balance through coverage diversity rather than pure pressure.

## Key Points

- Harbaugh's four reasons for hiring Macdonald: (1) organization experience, (2) part of the Ravens defensive culture-building, (3) integral to 2018 defensive rebuild, (4) proven at Michigan
- Harbaugh: Macdonald was "on the ground floor" of the 2018 Ravens defensive rebuild — so his promotion was continuity, not a gamble
- Macdonald's first press conference quote defining his philosophy: "The aggressiveness absolutely is going to carry over, but I think you've got to look through the lens of, 'What does aggressiveness actually mean?'"
- Aggression redefined: "A lot of times we'll come with a schemed-up pressure. That'll happen. A lot of times it could be a fake pressure. It could look like this coverage and play like another one."
- Core principle: "You want to be the one pushing the envelope rather than the other way around."
- This was Macdonald's philosophical pivot away from predecessor Wink Martindale's blitz-heavy, man-dominant style — keeping the spirit while changing the mechanism

## Newsletter Angles

- The first day framing: Macdonald's press conference answer about "what does aggression mean?" is the clearest articulation of his defensive philosophy in his own words — written before he'd coached a single game as DC. The same philosophy is now at the foundation of Seattle's Super Bowl defense
- Redefinition as leadership: the ability to say "we're keeping aggression" while changing what aggression means is a political and cultural skill — it lets you change the system without triggering identity-level resistance from players
