## Summary

WBAY profile of John Schneider surrounding the Seahawks' Super Bowl LX appearance. Schneider was named NFL Executive of the Year in his 16th season. Frames the achievement as validation of his complete roster rebuild philosophy built around Mike Macdonald's vision. The "first GM ever with zero player or coaching holdovers" framing should be read as "zero holdovers from the prior coaching regime" — see contradictions caveat above.

## Key Points

- First GM ever to lead a team to the Super Bowl with zero holdovers from any previous roster (player or coaching staff)
- 16th season as Seahawks GM; won Super Bowl in 2014 season (XLVIII); returning to Super Bowl in 2025 season (LX)
- Complete roster rebuild: released D.K. Metcalf and Tyler Lockett; signed Sam Darnold, Cooper Kupp, DeMarcus Lawrence
- Named NFL Executive of the Year 2026
- Partnership with Macdonald described as built on "clear vision, direct communication, aligned personnel philosophy"
- Roster built to execute Macdonald's defensive scheme; offense designed to complement not compete with defensive identity

## Newsletter Angles

- **The zero-holdover stat**: No other GM has ever done this. Schneider rebuilt an entire franchise from scratch — different players, different coaches, different scheme — and immediately reached the Super Bowl. This is the most extreme version of the "systems > stars" argument.
- **Continuity vs. rebuilding**: Most successful franchises win through continuity (Brady/Belichick, Mahomes/Reid). Schneider won through deliberate discontinuity. That's a different model worth studying.
- **The payoff of the Schneider essay**: [[Schneider Solved the Salary Cap While Everyone Else Complained]] was written when the Seahawks were 14-3 with massive cap space. This source is the payoff — the thesis played all the way out.
