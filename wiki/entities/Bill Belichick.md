## Overview

Head coach of the New England Patriots 2000–2023. Widely considered the greatest coach in NFL history. With Tom Brady at QB, won 6 Super Bowls and appeared in 9, building what analysts and historians recognize as the greatest dynasty in NFL history. His tenure ended after the 2023 season (his first without Brady) when he and the Patriots mutually parted ways.

## Key Facts

- Patriots record: 266–121 regular season, 17 division titles in 19 seasons, 6 Super Bowl wins, 9 appearances
- Defensive innovator: adjusted game plan week to week to neutralize the opponent's best asset; scheme flexibility over system rigidity
- Brady draft: used a compensatory 6th-round pick in 2000; one of the most consequential draft decisions in sports history
- First head coaching stint (Cleveland Browns, 1991–95): 36–44 record, one winning season; fired
- Post-Brady record (2023): 4–13; parted ways with Patriots after 24 seasons
- The Brady-Belichick split question: FiveThirtyEight analysis suggests neither alone fully explains the dynasty; interdependence was structural [[Brady vs. Belichick — Who's To Blame For The Patriots' Insufferable Success]]
