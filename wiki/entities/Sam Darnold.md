## Overview

Quarterback for the Seattle Seahawks. Career narrative: selected 3rd overall by the New York Jets in 2018, struggled under poor organizational conditions, released by the Carolina Panthers, resurrected with the Minnesota Vikings in 2024 (4,319 yards, 35 TDs), signed by Seattle on a 3-year, $100.5M deal in March 2025. Won Super Bowl LX in his first Seahawks season.

## Key Facts

- Signed 3-year, $100.5M contract with $55M guaranteed, March 2025 [[Seahawks Signing QB Sam Darnold to 3-Year $100.5 Million Contract]]
- 2024 Vikings season: 4,319 passing yards, 35 TDs — career best under OC Klint Kubiak
- Cap hit: $27.5M/year — expensive by normal standards, team-friendly by elite QB standards
- Reunited with Kubiak (49ers connection) in Seattle; scheme familiarity was a factor in the signing
- First player development arc: Jets failure → Panthers failure → Vikings renaissance → Seahawks Super Bowl

## Newsletter Relevance

**Power**: Darnold's career arc illustrates the organizational context thesis — the same player failed badly in bad organizations and thrived in well-run ones. His Jets tenure (2018-2020) coincided with organizational dysfunction; his Vikings and Seahawks tenures under structured systems produced completely different results.
