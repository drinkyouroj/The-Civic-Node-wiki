## Overview

Kevin Warsh is a former Federal Reserve Governor (2006–2011) and Wall Street figure widely discussed as a potential replacement for Jerome Powell as Fed Chair. He is considered by some analysts as more aligned with Trump's preference for lower rates than the current Fed consensus, making him a focal point in speculation about what a post-Powell Fed would look like.

## Key Facts

- Former Fed Governor 2006–2011; appointed by George W. Bush
- Has been publicly critical of the Fed's approach to monetary policy post-2008
- Named in reporting as a potential Trump choice to replace Powell when his chair term expires May 2026
- Trump also floated Christopher Waller and potentially others as replacements
- Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent noted an October–November nomination window for the next chair, to allow Senate confirmation [[Trump's tariffs kept Fed from cutting rates, Jerome Powell says]]

## Newsletter Relevance

**Monetary Policy**: Warsh represents the "what comes next" question. If Trump appoints a successor more sympathetic to aggressive rate cuts, the credibility the Fed has built under Powell resisting tariff-inflation pressure could unravel quickly.

**Politics/Power**: The Fed chair appointment is one of the most consequential economic levers a president holds. Warsh's potential nomination illustrates how executive pressure on the Fed doesn't require firing the chair — it just requires waiting out the term.
