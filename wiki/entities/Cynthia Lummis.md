## Overview

Senator Cynthia Lummis (R-WY) is the U.S. Senate's leading crypto champion — a Bitcoin maximalist who has held personal Bitcoin investments throughout her Senate tenure and has been the primary legislative architect of crypto-friendly legislation in the upper chamber. She facilitated bipartisan passage of the GENIUS Act (signed July 18, 2025) and has championed the Strategic Bitcoin Reserve concept. Wyoming, her state, has been the most crypto-forward state legislature in the U.S. for nearly a decade.

## Key Facts

- U.S. Senator from Wyoming; Republican.
- One of the most prominent Bitcoin holders in Congress; has disclosed personal BTC holdings.
- Key facilitator of bipartisan GENIUS Act passage in the Senate (June 17, 2025).
- Quote on Crypto Week: "For the first time in US history, we have a president who sees the value in embracing digital assets." [[Bitcoin soars past $120,000 ahead of Crypto Week]]
- Wyoming has developed the "Wyoming Stable Token" — first public-entity fiat-backed stablecoin in the U.S. — under her legislative influence at the state level.
- Advocate for the Strategic Bitcoin Reserve concept in Congress.
- Works closely with Senator Bill Hagerty (R-TN), sponsor of the GENIUS Act in the Senate.

## Newsletter Relevance

Lummis represents the political coalition that made crypto legislation happen: libertarian-leaning fiscal conservatives + tech optimists + Bitcoin believers. Her Wyoming Stable Token initiative shows a state government actually implementing what Congress has debated federally for years. She's also the proof case that personal financial interests (disclosed Bitcoin holdings) can coexist with legislative advocacy without a legal barrier — the same structure Democrats criticized in Trump's USD1 conflict of interest.
