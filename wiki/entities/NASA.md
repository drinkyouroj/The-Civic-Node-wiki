## Overview

NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration) is the U.S. federal agency responsible for civilian space exploration, aeronautics research, and Earth observation. Founded in 1958, it operates major programs including the Artemis lunar program (returning humans to the moon), the International Space Station, and Earth-monitoring satellite systems including Black Marble. As of April 2026, Artemis II has successfully flown a crewed mission around the moon.

## Key Facts

- Artemis II successfully looped a crew around the moon as of April 2026 — first crewed lunar proximity mission in approximately 50 years. [[Artemis II Conspiracy Theorists Already Failing — Vice]]
- Black Marble program: uses VIIRS sensor data from Suomi NPP and NOAA-20 satellites to monitor Earth's nighttime light at global scale. Foundational to light pollution research. [[Light Pollution Brightened Earth 16 Percent Since 2014 — Nature Study]]
- VIIRS sensor limitation: not sensitive to blue-wavelength LED light, which is the dominant modern street lighting technology. This creates a systematic underestimation of actual light pollution levels. [[Light Pollution Brightened Earth 16 Percent Since 2014 — Nature Study]]
