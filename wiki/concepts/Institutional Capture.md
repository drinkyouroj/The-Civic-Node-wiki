## Definition

Institutional capture is the process by which a regulatory body, oversight institution, or professional norm structure comes to serve the interests of the entity it is meant to constrain rather than the public. Capture can be staffing-based (the regulated industry supplies the regulator's personnel), procedural (rulemaking is dominated by the regulated party), or ideological (regulators internalize the regulated party's worldview).

## Why It Matters for the Newsletter

**Power**: Institutional capture is the slow-motion version of the more visible attacks on independence the wiki tracks elsewhere — the Trump pressure campaign on the Fed, the Bannon vacatur, the regulatory weaponization pattern. The blunt-force versions are dramatic but the captured-as-default state is the steady baseline against which the dramatic episodes have to be read.

## Tensions & Counterarguments

- "Capture" is often invoked rhetorically without rigorous evidence; not every regulatory friendliness is capture
- Some industry-regulator overlap is functionally necessary (you need expertise) and shades into capture only when the loop closes
- Strong public-interest constituencies can sometimes counter capture (consumer groups, plaintiffs' bar) — the question is whether they have parity of access
