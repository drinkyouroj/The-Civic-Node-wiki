## The Central Story (as of 4 sources)

Two simultaneous conflicts are revealing how state power operates against infrastructure — physical and digital.

**Conflict 1 — US-Iran War**: [[Iran]] closed the [[Strait of Hormuz]] after US-Israeli strikes began February 28, 2026. [[Donald Trump]] is using infrastructure ultimatums (power plants, bridges, desalination) and oil seizure threats. Iran is expanding the conflict: strikes on energy infrastructure across Kuwait, UAE, and Bahrain on April 5 signal this is no longer a bilateral conflict — it's a regional infrastructure war. Active backchannel negotiations continue beneath the public confrontation.

**Conflict 2 — US-Anthropic**: The Trump administration blacklisted [[Anthropic]] for refusing to allow [[Claude]] to be used for military surveillance or autonomous weapons. The [[US Department of Defense]] used a "supply-chain risk" designation — a regulatory weapon — against a non-compliant private company. [[United Kingdom]] is now recruiting Anthropic. A US judge temporarily blocked the blacklist.

Both conflicts share the same deep structure: **centralized infrastructure and the state's will to control it**.

## Active Themes

### Power & Infrastructure
[[Chokepoint Control]] and [[Infrastructure Warfare]] are the dominant patterns across the Iran story. The Strait is the physical paradigm; Anthropic's AI infrastructure is the digital one. In both cases, the state is attempting to control or destroy infrastructure that won't comply with its demands.

### Monetary Policy
[[War-Driven Inflation]] is live: gas up ~37% in 5 weeks. Iran's expansion of strikes to Gulf energy sites (Kuwait, UAE, Bahrain) raises the regional risk premium. This is a supply shock the Fed cannot address with rate tools.

### Politics
